02
How I Work

Pre-production, on-site, and post, treated as one piece.

Architectural photography lives or dies in preparation. By the time the shoot day arrives, we already know what we're shooting and why. The day itself is execution against a plan we've built together.

i
Before the Day
  • Onboarding questionnaire. The primary tool. It captures the site's narrative, what matters in each space, why specific design moves were made, and which moments need to read in the imagery.
  • Drawings and reference material. Plans, elevations, sections, renderings, mood images. Anything you can share so I know what the building is before I see it.
  • Sun-path study against the site orientation, so we know which elevation gets which light at which hour.
  • Optional alignment call. Welcome but not required. The questionnaire usually does the work. A call is for projects with unusual constraints or tight timing.
ii
On the Day
  • Tilt-shift workflow for vertical perspective control. Lines stay true at the camera, not in post. The building's geometry is preserved.
  • Tethered capture for color-critical interior work. What you see on the laptop is what arrives in the final file.
  • Off-camera light shaping where interior conditions need filling, applied so the image still reads as natural light.
  • Drone work for site context and roof plane. Transport Canada Advanced certified, regulations and weather permitting.
  • Quiet on site. Trades, owners, or your client team accommodated, not directed.
iii
After the Day
  • Each frame processed individually. Color, tone, and contrast set per image, not against a global preset.
  • Verticals, horizons, and cropping checked frame-by-frame.
  • Custom crops prepared on request for any platform or use case with specific aspect requirements.
  • Final delivery in 10 to 15 business days. Selects available within 72 hours if a deadline is in play.
Tools
Tilt-shift lenses, tethered capture, studio strobes, Transport Canada Advanced drone certification, calibrated end-to-end colour workflow.
03
Through the Day

How a Salt Spring shoot day actually unfolds.

Late June and early July give roughly sixteen hours of usable light on the south coast. Times below are approximate and adjust to the residence's specific orientation, which we lock during the pre-shoot questionnaire.

05:30 to 07:00

First light, walk-through, east elevations.

Gear set, walk the residence with you or your team to confirm the day's order. East and south-east facing elevations as the sun comes up. The building's quietest face, often its strongest.

07:00 to 10:00

Morning interiors with east exposure.

Interior spaces that take morning light directly. The softest natural-light window of the day. Tethered capture so we can read color and tone live.

10:00 to 14:00

Detail, material, and aerials.

Interior detail studies and material moments. Joinery, transitions, hardware, surface. Drone work mid-window when sun height gives a clean read on roof planes and site context, conditions allowing.

14:00 to 17:30

West elevations and second-pass interiors.

West-facing elevations as light begins to direct. Second pass on key interior spaces with afternoon light, often a different read than the morning frame of the same room.

17:30 to 20:00

Golden hour, hero exteriors.

The angles that need warm directional light. Long-shadow elevations, approach and threshold frames, the building reading against late-afternoon sky.

20:00 to 21:30

Twilight, the strongest exterior frame.

Interior lights on, exteriors against deep blue sky. We hold for the four to eight minutes when ambient and interior brightness balance, and we work fast. The building reads in a way it doesn't at any other hour.

21:30 onward

Backup, cull notes, wrap.

Files backed to two drives on site before gear is packed. Cull notes captured while the day is still fresh, so the morning culling session is already half-done.

Note

Twilight is the only window when interior light and exterior architecture can be read in the same frame. We'll plan the day to protect it, weather pending.

04
Deliverables

What arrives at the end.

Final selects are processed one at a time, frame by frame. Below is what gets delivered.

  • Final image count. 25 to 35 finished images, fully retouched and color-balanced. Coverage spans exteriors at multiple times of day, interiors, material detail, site context, and twilight.
  • File formats. High-resolution print files (uncompressed JPEG) and web-optimized files for digital use, delivered side by side.
  • Custom crops. Specific aspect ratios prepared on request for any platform or use case where you need them.
  • Drone captures. Site context aerials and roof plane reads where regulations and weather allow. Delivered as part of the final set, no separate fee.
  • Delivery. Dropbox folder, organized for direct hand-off to your team. Available indefinitely.
  • Turnaround. 10 to 15 business days from shoot date. Selects within 72 hours if a deadline requires it.

Worth Knowing

A note on cost share.

Most projects like this get structured as a cost share. The shoot fee is split between parties with a shared interest in the imagery, typically the architect, the interior designer, and the sub-trades whose work shows up in the building.

Matt invoices the architect the full grand total. The architect distributes the cost-share to each contributing vendor. The architect's net out-of-pocket is the difference. The math, the worked example, and the billing flow are all below.

The Math

A 30% uplift is added to the photography fee for each vendor added. The new total is divided across all parties. Travel days and travel costs sit outside the cost share, billed to the architect.

How the imagery gets used.

Rights are extended for agreed purposes: your firm's website, marketing, and any submissions or pitches you control directly. Third-party use by other vendors or collaborators routes through me for approval and licensing on a per-use basis. I retain copyright on the underlying work.

Booking & Payment

50% deposit on signing to hold the date. Balance plus travel actuals invoiced on delivery, net 15. Payment via e-transfer or direct deposit to Matt Anthony Photography.

Weather & Reschedule

Salt Spring weather in late June and early July is generally workable, but conditions can shift. We'll confirm go/no-go 48 hours out. If we reschedule for weather, the deposit rolls to the new date with no penalty.

Coordination Window

Once the date is locked, I block the surrounding days for travel and weather buffer. I ask for at least three weeks of lead time on confirmed shoot dates so the schedule and ferries align.
