What’s Strong (and Rare)

  • Content scores 100/100. 22 project pages with descriptions. 645-word homepage. Blog with 5 posts and a lead magnet. This is the best content foundation I’ve audited in the Fraser Valley.
  • Visual assets score 92/100. 1,336 images, 97.5% with proper alt text. Project pages average 53.8 images each. Outstanding coverage.
  • CHBA Fraser Valley award winner. 2024 Housing Awards of Excellence for Modern Farmhouse renovation. Real credential that competitors don’t have.
  • Directory presence. Houzz, BBB, and TrustedPros profiles active. Multiple citation sources in place.

The Specific Gaps

  • Zero video content. No project films, no reels, no walkthrough videos, no before/after reveals. This is the single biggest content gap. Renovation transformation video is the highest-performing format for design-build firms, and Copper Creek has none.
  • No structured data (schema.org). Zero JSON-LD markup. Google doesn’t know Copper Creek is a builder or where you operate. One-time fix with permanent impact.
  • No Google Business Profile detected. Can’t appear in Google Maps or the Local Pack. With 22 projects, 18 service areas, and a CHBA award, this would be one of the strongest GBP profiles in the Fraser Valley.
  • The award story is underutilized. One mention on the site. No dedicated case study, no social content celebrating the win, no credential badges on project pages. One award should generate months of content.

The Bottom Line

Copper Creek’s website and content are ahead of the competition. This isn’t a fix-what’s-broken situation. It’s an activate-what-you-have situation. The gaps are specific: video, build documentation, GBP, and leveraging the award. A content partner fills exactly these holes.
