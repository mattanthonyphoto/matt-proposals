GD Nielsen Homes — Digital Audit

Prepared for the discovery call, April 7, 2026

1. Snapshot

GD Nielsen Homes is a 40-year-old luxury custom builder ($1.5M–$10M jobs) in North Vancouver, with a deep portfolio of 26 West Van waterfront and view homes — but a thin, dated, Squarespace marketing layer that dramatically undersells the work. They have real award credentials (Georgie wins, Avid Custom Builder, BBB A+), but their website tells almost no project stories, their Instagram is dormant, they have zero Houzz reviews and only 4 Google reviews, and on the photography front they have a clear interior-coverage gap that is leaving Georgie- and publication-grade projects invisible. Significant low-hanging fruit for both a photography retainer and a website rebuild.

Content / Project Page Depth

Second-biggest gap.

Design credits sporadic: - Cammeray Heights → Craig Chevalier Custom Home Designs + Oy Christopher Design - Casa at the Cove → Craig Chevalier Designs + Living Radius - Howe Sound Haven → Alex Voth Design + Oy Christopher Design - Kerrisdale Zen → Kallweit Graham Architecture - Vista Estate → Rommel Design + Oy Christopher - Lions Gate Vista → Oy Christopher - The other 19 projects list no architect or designer at all — massive missed referral and SEO opportunity.

Award call-outs also inconsistent. Cammeray Heights, Kerrisdale Zen, Lions Gate Vista, and Vista Estate name specific Georgie/Ovation status. The other 22 projects (including Forested Family Estate, which won the Georgie for Best Single Family Kitchen over $100K) don't.

SEO

  • Page <title> is just "GD Nielsen Homes " (with a trailing space, no keyword) — terrible
  • No <meta name="description"> tag found on the homepage. Squarespace is auto-generating a fallback. Fixable own-goal.
  • No JSON-LD / schema.org structured data at all. No LocalBusiness, no Organization, no BreadcrumbList. Leaving Google rich-result eligibility on the table.
  • Open Graph image is the company logo on white — not a hero project — so every social share looks weak.
  • Sitemap is healthy (89KB, lists all pages).
  • Mandarin pages exist with no hreflang annotations.
  • The duplicate /home-3 page is an indexable orphan.

Technical

  • Squarespace = solid performance baseline, mobile responsive by default
  • Image loading acceptable (homepage 128KB HTML)
  • No obvious broken links in sitemap crawl
  • Alt text on most project images likely empty (Squarespace default; would need page-level confirmation)

7. Website Rebuild — What's Needed

A new gdnielsen.com should add:

  1. Real project pages with narrative (300–500 words), site/program/material story, square footage, year, location, full credit block (architect, interior designer, landscape, photographer), and award flags
  2. Filterable portfolio — by location (West Van / North Van / Vancouver / Whistler), style (modern, traditional, French, shingle, waterfront), budget tier
  3. Team page with bios for Greg, DK, Diane, Derrick, plus key superintendents
  4. Press / Publications page to capture features going forward
  5. Testimonials page with real client stories pulled from the Avid 100% recommendation data and the existing Rick & Shemin / Barb & Jeff quotes on the Story page
  6. Process page rebuild — current 4-step copy is generic; should include build timeline, what to expect month by month, photos of actual sites
  7. Lead capture upgrades — consultation booking link, "request a portfolio PDF" magnet, qualifier fields (budget, lot status, timeline)
  8. SEO foundation — proper titles/meta descriptions, LocalBusiness + Organization + BreadcrumbList schema, alt text on every image, hreflang for the Mandarin pages OR removal, deletion of /home-3 orphan
  9. Award years and dating — every award labeled with year and project
  10. Architect/designer SEO co-mentions — naming Craig Chevalier, Kallweit Graham, Alex Voth, Oy Christopher, Living Radius, Rommel on relevant project pages turns those pages into referral magnets and inbound link bait
  11. Open Graph hero images per page, not the logo
  12. Either kill or finish the Mandarin section

8. Top 7 Gaps a Photographer + Website Partner Could Fix (Priority Order)

  1. ~10 award-caliber projects have no interior coverage on the site. Including their Georgie kitchen winner. The #1 issue and the most natural pitch: re-shoot interiors on 6–10 priority projects (or shoot 2026 builds in full) so the portfolio finally matches the work.
  2. Zero photographer credits anywhere — symptom of an unstable photography supply chain. A retainer relationship fixes this permanently.
  3. No project narratives — every project page reads like a real estate listing without the listing. Copywriting bundle or paired writer.
  4. Houzz has zero reviews and zero "Best of Houzz" badges for a 40-year builder. Reputational malpractice. Fixable via a structured review-request campaign tied to new project deliveries.
  5. Instagram dormant since ~March 2023 while chief competitor posts daily and just won Georgie Builder of the Year. A quarterly photography retainer naturally fuels a content engine.
  6. No team page / bios — invisible humans behind a luxury purchase decision.
  7. SEO basics — missing meta descriptions, missing schema, generic titles, duplicate /home-3 page, half-built Chinese mirror with no hreflang. All quick wins.

9. Top 4 Hooks for the Sales Conversation

Hook 1 — The Georgie kitchen they don't show

"You've got at least one Georgie-winning project showing only a single exterior photo on your own website."

Forested Family Estate, Best Single Family Kitchen over $100K — and there is no kitchen photo on the project page. Single most quantifiable gap. Perfect way to shift the conversation from "we need photos" to "you have already-built award winners that are completely invisible online."

Hook 2 — The competitive frame

"Hasler Homes just won 2026 Georgie Custom Home Builder of the Year with 9,500 Instagram followers and 1,700 posts. You're at well under 1,000 IG followers and your last reel was March 2023."

Makes urgency real without trashing them — they're being outmarketed, not outbuilt.

Hook 3 — The reputation gap

"You've got a 40-year reputation, an Avid 100% recommendation score, and zero Houzz reviews and only 4 Google reviews surfacing publicly."

A photographer + website partner can build the asset library and the content engine that finally captures the social proof their reputation has earned.

Hook 4 — The referral flywheel

"You already work with great architects and designers — Craig Chevalier, Kallweit Graham, Alex Voth, Oy Christopher, Living Radius, Rommel — and only 6 of your 26 project pages credit them."

Crediting them is free referral marketing. Photographing the next round of joint projects is the relationship lubricant that turns "they built our house" into "we recommend them to every client." The Creative Partner story in one line.

10. Estimated Engagement Value

The strongest combined photo + website opportunity in the current pipeline. They've already raised both topics on the call invite, the budget is clearly there ($1.5M–$10M jobs per Houzz), and the marketing gap is the largest of any luxury NV builder.

Recommendation for tomorrow: Anchor the retainer ($2.5K–$3.5K/mo) as the centerpiece because it solves the supply-chain problem (no more orphaned project shoots) and bundle the website rebuild as a one-time Q2/Q3 project. Don't lead with website rebuild alone — they need the photography asset library first or any new website launches with the same broken portfolio.
