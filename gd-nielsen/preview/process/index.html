01

Phase 01

Approximately 1 month

Discovery.

A site visit, an honest conversation about the brief, and a first pass at what the project is going to cost. We use the month to figure out whether we’re the right team for the job — and whether the job is the right one for us.

What happens

  • Site walk with Greg, ideally on the lot
  • Brief alignment — program, scale, finish level
  • Initial budget framing in real West Vancouver numbers
  • Fit check on both sides — calendar, scope, working style

Deliverables

  • Written project brief
  • Preliminary budget range
  • A clear decision on whether to engage

What we ask you for

  • The lot or address
  • Your vision — in whatever form it lives right now
  • Any architect you’re already working with
  • The budget band you’re planning around

What you get

An honest, numbers-based read on the project before either side commits. If we’re not the right fit — calendar, scope, location — we say so in this phase, not after a contract is signed.

02

Phase 02

Approximately 5 months

Design & Permits.

We work alongside the architect from schematic through construction documents, price the work with our long-standing trades, and shepherd the District of West Vancouver permit through review. The phase ends with a building permit, a signed contract, and a budget that holds.

What happens

  • Architect engagement — we work with the architect you’ve chosen
  • Schematic design → design development → construction documents
  • Geotechnical investigation and survey, if the lot calls for them
  • Trade pricing against the CD set, with our long-standing partners
  • Permit application and District of West Vancouver review
  • Finalized budget reconciled against the issued permit

Deliverables

  • Full construction document set
  • Issued building permit
  • Signed contractor agreement and finalized budget

How we work with the architect

From the architect’s drawings, not against them. Where a detail needs a constructability conversation, it goes back to the architect before anything changes on site. We don’t substitute spec mid-project to make a margin.

Why this phase pays back

Decisions made on paper cost a fraction of decisions made on a framed wall. The five months we spend in this phase is the cheapest time you’ll spend on the project.

Note · West Vancouver permitting

District of West Vancouver permit review runs roughly 8 to 16 weeks for complex applications — view bylaws, FAR math, Step Code, and geotechnical triggers all factor in. Forty years working with the District means we know what each reviewer is looking for and we stage submissions accordingly.

03

Phase 03

12 to 18 months

Build.

Twelve to eighteen months on site, broken into six predictable sub-phases. Greg is on the site, Derrick runs the schedule, Wes runs the trades on the ground. You’re kept current through weekly site visits, monthly draws, and decision packages at every milestone.

Sub-phases

  • Months 1–3 Foundation & framing
  • Months 3–6 Envelope & roof
  • Months 5–9 Mechanical, electrical, plumbing rough-in
  • Months 8–14 Insulation, drywall & interior finishes
  • Months 12–18 Cabinetry, millwork, tile & hardware
  • Final stage Inspections & deficiency walk

Your touchpoints

  • Weekly site visits — with Greg, on the work
  • Monthly draws against scheduled progress
  • Decision packages at every milestone
  • Direct access to Greg, Derrick, and Wes throughout

What stays constant

The same trade partners from foundation through handover — Andy on framing, Tom on electrical, Peter on finishing — fifteen to twenty years apiece. Continuity at every stage is what lets the architect’s detail survive.

04

Phase 04

Approximately 3 months

Handover.

The work doesn’t end at occupancy. We walk the home with you, resolve the deficiency list, enrol the home in BC 2-5-10 warranty, and come back twelve months later for a warranty walk while the house has had a year to settle.

What happens

  • Pre-occupancy walkthrough with Greg & the trade leads
  • Deficiency list captured, scheduled, and closed out
  • BC 2-5-10 New Home Warranty enrolment
  • Final occupancy and key handover
  • One-year warranty walk after the home has settled through a full season

Deliverables

  • BC 2-5-10 warranty package
  • All appliance, mechanical and equipment manuals
  • A finish-spec book — every material on the home, on the record
  • Plant guide, if the project included landscape scope

What you receive

A buildable, warranted, documented home. The spec book and warranty paperwork live with you — the next time a tap, a trim profile, or a paint colour needs a touch, the answer is on the shelf.

What stays open

The relationship. Most of our clients come back — for a second home, an addition, a landscape phase, or simply to call when something needs a hand. We’re built to be a long-term builder, not a one-project transaction.

Process FAQ

Four things architects and clients ask about process.

If your question isn’t here, ask it on the first call — we’d rather work through it in conversation than leave it for later.

  • When should we engage you in the process?

    Early. We do our best work when we’re in conversation with the architect during design development, not handed a finished construction document set to price. The earlier we’re at the table, the more constructability we can bake in before pricing locks — and the fewer surprises everyone runs into in the field.

  • Do you provide architectural design?

    No. We work from the architect’s drawings. If you don’t already have an architect, we can suggest a short list of practices we’ve built for and trust — but the architect’s relationship is yours, not ours.

  • What happens if costs exceed the budget mid-project?

    We communicate early. Every project carries a contingency line — we recommend twelve percent. If a true scope change comes up, it goes through a written change order before any trade picks up a tool. We don’t surprise clients with line items at closeout, and we don’t hide a price drift behind “allowances” the way some builders do.

  • Can we tour an active site?

    Yes, with proper notice. Active sites are working environments — we need lead time to coordinate with the client we’re currently building for, brief our trades, and make sure we can show you the work safely. Mention it on the first call and we’ll arrange it.
