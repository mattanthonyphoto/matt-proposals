What we renovate

A typical renovation, by the numbers.

Most of our renovations are on existing West Vancouver homes between twenty-five hundred and five thousand square feet — mid-century, eighties-build, even nineteen-twenties heritage. Often the family is already living elsewhere for the duration.

Scope varies. Some are whole-home, walls down to studs. Some are major-system — new envelope, new mechanical, new kitchen, but keeping the bones. The price range below reflects that spread.

The renovation difference

Why renovations are harder than new builds.

On a new build, the slab is poured to spec and the framing goes up the way the engineer drew it. On a renovation, you open up a wall and find a beam that’s an inch off, knob-and-tube in the attic, an old radiant loop nobody documented, and a header that was load-bearing the whole time.

The first three months of any serious West Vancouver renovation are an investigation as much as a build. Selective demolition, exploratory openings, locating services. Then matching old to new — floor heights, ceiling lines, baseboard returns, where the new drywall meets the original plaster.

What that means in practice: a higher contingency than a new build, a deliberate sequence of investigations before lock-in, and a builder who’s seen it before. Forty years on the North Shore is the only honest qualification for it.
