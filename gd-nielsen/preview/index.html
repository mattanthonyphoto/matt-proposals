Frequently Asked

Five things people ask before they hire.

  • What does GD Nielsen Homes build?

    Custom homes, whole-home and major-system renovations, tear-down rebuilds, second-story additions and multi-generational suites, and outdoor living spaces. Custom home construction is the anchor service. See all services

  • Where does GD Nielsen Homes build?

    West Vancouver primary — the majority of our portfolio projects are West Van builds, across Altamont, Caulfeild, the British Properties, Chartwell, Cammeray, Cypress Park, and Dundarave. We also build across North Vancouver, Vancouver, and Greater Vancouver — including Burnaby, Richmond, Langley, White Rock, South Surrey, and Tsawwassen. For large-budget custom homes anywhere in the Lower Mainland, get in touch.

  • Will you build to the architect’s drawings without value-engineering them?

    Yes. We work from the construction documents as drawn — millwork details, mechanical layouts, envelope assemblies. Where a detail needs a constructability conversation we’ll bring it back to the architect before we change anything on site. We don’t substitute spec mid-project to make a margin.

  • How long does a custom home build take?

    About twenty-two months from first conversation to keys. Roughly six months pre-construction (discovery, architect, design, permits) and twelve to eighteen months for construction. West Vancouver permit timelines run eight to sixteen weeks for complex applications. See our process

  • What does a custom home cost in West Vancouver?

    Construction is approximately $600 to $900 per square foot in 2026, with most projects landing between $700 and $800. A 5,000 sq ft custom home is typically $3M to $4.5M for build alone, before land, soft costs (roughly 12% of build), and contingency (we recommend 12%). Read the cost guide
