Segment 02

The brand story

20 min · Greg leads · recorded

This is the longest segment. If Greg is in the room, lean on him here. DK fills the gaps; Greg has the 40-year memory.

Permission to record: "Mind if I record this so I don't have to scribble? I'll transcribe it and pull quotes for the site — you'll see every quote before it goes live."

Topic 1 — Origin · 5 min

Walk me through 1986. How did the first build happen?
Listen for: the first crew, the mentor, the project budget, the original office at 2405 Bellevue, the original name (G.D. Nielsen Developments Ltd.)

Topic 2 — The signature move · 5 min

When an architect calls and says "I have a tough one," why do they call you?
Listen for: technical chops on waterfront / sloped sites, view-protection bylaw navigation, direct trade relationships, in-house crew vs. broker model

Topic 3 — The hard one · 3 min

Tell me about the most difficult project you ever finished. The one that almost didn't happen.
This generates the war-story that makes the /story page feel alive.

Topic 4 — The repeat clients · 3 min

Who has hired you twice? Three times? Generationally?
Listen for family-compound stories, kids hiring you for their first home, multi-decade trust.

Topic 5 — Why only 1–2 builds a year · 2 min

Small Volume Builder of the Year — Georgie 2005. That's a deliberate choice. Why?
Frame it as scarcity-of-craft, not capacity ceiling.

Topic 6 — The future · 2 min

What does GDN look like in 2035? Is the family carrying it? The team?
Sensitive. Don't push if Greg shifts uncomfortably. But the answer is gold for the /story page closer.

Segment 08

Brand voice

10 min · capture verbatim

We need the vocabulary you actually use when describing your work. Builders rarely write down their own voice — this is where most builder websites sound like every other builder.

1. Describe a GD Nielsen home in one sentence. Not marketing — what would you tell a friend at a barbecue? 2a. Three words you reach for when describing your work
2b. Three words you actively avoid
3. When a client refers you to a friend, what do they say about you?

Voice direction (proposed)

  • Quiet confidence, not bombast. "We build homes that last" not "we craft masterpieces."
  • 40 years of evidence does the bragging — copy stays under-stated.
  • First person plural ("we") not third person ("GD Nielsen Homes Ltd. constructs…").
  • No "luxury" in every sentence. Use it once, where it matters.
Adjust this direction
