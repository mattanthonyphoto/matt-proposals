Where You Are

The work is top-tier.
The site isn't.

Greg, I dug into GD Nielsen Homes before our call — your 26 project pages, the awards page, all four social channels, your Houzz profile, your Google reviews, and the top luxury builders you compete with in NV/WV. Here's the honest read.

Forty years. Twenty-six West Vancouver waterfront and view homes. Project budgets from $1.5M to $10M. A Georgie Award for Best Single Family Kitchen over $100,000 on Forested Family Estate. Multiple Ovation wins. The Avid Custom Builder award for 100% client recommendation. CHBA Best Home Builder in BC 2005. BBB A+ since 1992. The built work is in the top three luxury builders in North Van by every measure that matters.

Then there's the website. Your Georgie kitchen winner has no kitchen photo on its own page. About 10 of your 26 projects show only a single exterior shot. 23 of 26 have no descriptive narrative beyond "Location: West Vancouver." Zero photographer credits anywhere. Zero square footage on any project. Zero completion year. Your Houzz profile has 26 projects and zero reviews. Your Instagram's last reel was March 2023. Hasler Homes — the closest direct competitor — just won 2026 Georgie Custom Home Builder of the Year with 9,572 Instagram followers and 1,766 posts. They're not building better houses. They're presenting them better.

The gap between your real reputation and your digital reputation is the largest one I've measured in the entire NV/WV competitive set. Closing it requires two things in this order: build the photography asset library the site has been missing for years, then rebuild the website around it. Do them together with one partner and the math gets significantly better.
