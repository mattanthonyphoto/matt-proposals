Our Story

Named After the Orca

In the early 2000s, David Adair spent his days guiding ocean kayak tours through Johnstone Strait, watching orcas move in tight family pods -- communicating constantly, protecting their young, navigating together. When he founded Blackfish Homes in 2005, the name wasn't a brand exercise. It was a statement about how he wanted to build: with the same focus on family, communication, and trust.

Two decades later, that philosophy hasn't changed. David now chairs the Built Green Canada Board of Directors -- the national organization certifying sustainable building practices across the country. Every Blackfish home is third-party verified for energy performance, material health, and durability. Not because it's trendy. Because it's the right way to build.

Built Green Certified

Not Just Green-Washed.Third-Party Verified.

Every Blackfish home is certified through Built Green Canada -- the national standard for sustainable residential construction. David Adair doesn't just build to the standard. He chairs the organization's Board of Directors.

Platinum Certification

Our flagship projects achieve Built Green Platinum -- the highest level of certification for energy performance, material health, and indoor air quality.

EnerGuide Rated

Our homes are independently tested and rated. The Vale residence scored EnerGuide 86 -- over 30% more efficient than building code requirements.
