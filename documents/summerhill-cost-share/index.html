How the photography cost-share works, and what it costs.

A short brief for vendors and trade partners deciding whether to participate in the upcoming Summerhill shoot. Three pages of plain math, no surprises.

01

What you receive when you opt in.

Deliverables

Each participating vendor gets the full image set, not a partial selection. The same files Summerhill receives are the files you receive.

  • Full image set. High-resolution files of the residence, web-ready and print-ready. Delivered together when the shoot is processed.
  • Full usage rights. Marketing, portfolio, website, social, print, awards, sales decks, manufacturer co-op submissions. Used wherever you'd use any other portfolio image.
  • Credit at your discretion. Credit photography to Matt Anthony where credit is given. No requirement to credit on every use.
  • One licensing relationship. Your participation covers your team's use in perpetuity. No re-licensing fees later for the same imagery.
02

The math, in one sentence.

Mechanism

Each additional vendor adds 30% to the photography fee. The new total is divided equally across everyone participating. The result, every time, is that adding more participants makes it cheaper for everyone already in.

04

How billing works.

Flow

Summerhill is the single point of contact for money. Licensing runs on a parallel track, directly between you and me.

  • Step one. Summerhill confirms the participant list before the shoot. That fixes your per-vendor share.
  • Step two. I send a short licensing agreement directly to each participating vendor for signature. It covers image usage, term, and credit. Standard one-page document.
  • Step three. Shoot happens. After delivery, I invoice Summerhill for the full grand total.
  • Step four. Summerhill invoices each participating vendor for their share plus 5% GST. Payment terms run through Summerhill on their standard schedule.
  • Step five. Once your license is signed and your invoice is settled with Summerhill, your team has full usage of the image set as described in section 01.

Travel time and travel costs sit outside the cost-share. Those are carried by Summerhill on the project side. Your share covers the photography pool only.

05

What we need from you.

To confirm

Three things from your side, all short.

  • Confirm participation with Kyle by reply, before the participant list is locked. Earlier confirmations help everyone, since each added vendor lowers the per-vendor share.
  • Send a short product or scope list for the residence. The specific windows, the millwork rooms, the tile installs, the finish areas. One paragraph or a quick list is enough. This tells me what to feature so the imagery serves your portfolio as well as Summerhill's.
  • Sign the licensing agreement. I'll send a short standard document once Summerhill confirms the participant list. It covers image usage rights for your team's marketing, portfolio, awards, and sales material. Signed copy back by email is all that's needed.

That's the full ask. Once the participant list closes, Summerhill issues the participation confirmation with the final per-vendor number, and the shoot proceeds on the scheduled date.
