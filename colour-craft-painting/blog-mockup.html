2026 Colour of the Year: What the Experts Picked and What It Means for Your Home

Every year, the major paint brands announce their colour of the year. In 2026, there's a clear direction: the industry is moving away from cool grays and stark whites toward warm earth tones, deep browns, and nature-inspired hues.

Benjamin Moore: Silhouette (AF-655)

A rich espresso brown with charcoal undertones. It's the clearest signal yet that the "quiet luxury" movement is deepening into richer, darker territory. Silhouette works as an accent wall, a full room drench, or on cabinetry where you want depth without going black.

Where to use it: Home offices, dining rooms, powder rooms, kitchen islands. Pair with warm whites like Chantilly Lace for contrast, or use it as trim against lighter walls for a grounded, architectural feel.

Sherwin-Williams: Universal Khaki (SW 6150)

A warm, midtone neutral that bridges beige and taupe. Part of their "Tailored & Timeless" forecast. Pairs beautifully with mossy green, deep red, butter yellow, and creamy white. This is the safe-but-sophisticated choice for anyone moving away from gray.

Dulux: Rhythm of Blues

For the first time, Dulux chose an entire colour family instead of a single shade. Three blues: Free Groove (bold royal navy), Mellow Flow (pale blue-gray), and Slow Swing (deep ocean blue). The message: blue isn't going anywhere, but it's getting warmer and more nuanced.

What This Means for BC Homeowners

If you're planning a refresh in 2026, the direction is clear: warmer, earthier, and more intentional. The days of painting every room Repose Gray are over. Whether you go bold with an espresso feature wall or subtle with a warm khaki throughout, the trend is toward colours that feel grounded and lived-in.
