03 — Audit
Findings

Three findings from the data that matter most.

i

Munter owns the searches that should be yours

On the keyword set a luxury East Kootenay buyer actually types — custom home builder Kimberly, luxury homes Cranbrook, timber frame builder Kootenays, passive house Kootenay, architect-led home builder Kimberly — Munter ranks in the top 3 on the majority. You rank outside the top 10 on all of them, and on several you don't appear on page 1 at all.

This isn't because Munter builds better houses. It's because their site is structured for search, and yours isn't.

ii

The website isn't telling Google — or ChatGPT — where you build

There's no location-specific service page, no dedicated "Kimberly custom home builder" page, no "East Kootenay luxury builder" page, no city-keyed content. Search engines can't rank you for searches you haven't told them you serve. Neither can the generative tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) that are increasingly where high-net-worth buyers start their research.

iii

Your content velocity is zero

Your blog hasn't published in 18+ months. Your "In Progress" social series stopped 149 weeks ago. You have no LinkedIn presence at all. For a builder of your caliber, your digital footprint should be one of your biggest sales assets — right now it's a liability.

04 — The Plan
In priority order

Not everything is equally urgent. Here's the order, and why.

01 Fix the conversion Months 1–2

Before we drive more traffic, we stop wasting the traffic you already have.

  • Website rebuild on a 2026-standard structure. Location established in the first three seconds. Service-location pages for Kimberly, Cranbrook, Fernie, Invermere, Golden. GEO-ready structure for ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity citation. Interlinked pages. Page-speed optimized.
  • Image SEO done right. Every photo renamed with descriptive, search-optimized filenames. Alt text written for every image — critical for Google Images, accessibility, and generative search citation.
  • Portfolio overhaul. Four to five of your best finished and near-finished projects shot in one bundled Kootenay trip. Portfolio rebuilt around that imagery. Current imagery is good; new imagery will be remarkable.
  • Migration plan. I handle the Tamarack transition — domain retrieval, DNS cutover, analytics preservation, zero-downtime launch.
02 Beat Munter Months 2–6

Now we go after the rankings.

  • Keyword + GEO strategy. Target the 20 highest-intent terms in your market. Publish content that claims each one.
  • Two long-form blog posts per month, written in your voice using the brand-voice extraction system, optimized for both Google and generative search.
  • Monthly new page publishing — each month adds a new service page, location page, or pillar post. Twelve months from now, your site has 24+ new indexed pages targeting Munter's keyword set.
  • Rank tracking + monthly reporting. You see exactly where you rank against Munter week-over-week.
03 Build the distribution engine Months 3–6

Once the foundation holds, we turn on distribution.

  • LinkedIn launch. Company page, thought-leadership content for you, project features, behind-the-scenes. Your buyers are on LinkedIn. Your competitors aren't using it well. Open lane.
  • Content pillars workshop. Month 1 deliverable: we define four to six content pillars for your brand (Craft, Legacy, Process, Place, People). Every piece of content after ladders to one.
  • Instagram revival. "In Progress" series back on. Stories tied to active projects. Highlights properly curated. Feed pacing defined.
  • Local photographer brief (optional). I provide a pillar-mapped shot list. You source a Kootenay-based photographer for fill content between my shoot trips. Keeps the feed alive.

  • Avenue Calgary & Avenue Edmonton — direct line to your Alberta buyers

05 Lead tracking Month 1, ongoing

10 — Transition
Leaving Tamarack cleanly

Zero downtime. The ads don't go dark.

You're in a contract with Tamarack and don't know who owns your domain. Your Google and Meta campaigns are running through them. Here's exactly how I handle all of it.

The website migration

  • Before anything else: one-page checklist to forward to Justina or Tamarack — domain ownership, DNS access, asset handoff, account logins.
  • Parallel build. New Squarespace site built on a staging URL. Your live site never stops serving visitors while we build.
  • Contract timing. Tell me when Tamarack ends; I time launch to the day after. Zero overlap in fees.
  • Cutover in one afternoon. DNS change, SSL verification, analytics preservation, 301 redirects for any URL that's changing. Site is never offline more than minutes.
  • Handoff protocol. If Tamarack owns any assets (domain, image files, account logins), we retrieve them before launch. I have a script for that conversation.

The paid ads handoff

Tamarack currently runs your Google and Meta campaigns. When they leave, those campaigns need a home — or they go dark. I handle this three ways, and we decide together in Month 1.

  • Audit first. Month 1: full audit of existing Google & Meta campaigns. Spend, CTR, cost per click, conversion data (what little exists without UTM). You see what's actually working before we touch anything.
  • Path A — Pause and reinvest. For most builders in your position, I recommend pausing paid ads for 90 days while SEO, GEO, and content velocity come online. Redirect the budget into one of the retainer tiers or reserve it. Restart paid once the foundation can convert the traffic.
  • Path B — Keep running with a specialist partner. If the audit shows strong ROI worth protecting, I hand off campaign management to a trusted paid-media partner (pre-vetted, Alberta-geo-aware, on a flat monthly fee you pay directly). I oversee creative and messaging; they optimize spend.
  • Path C — Narrow the spend. If the audit is mixed, we cut campaigns to the highest-performing assets only and run them leaner while the organic side ramps.

You will never be in a situation where ads are running unmanaged. That's the commitment.

You do not lose a day of visibility during the transition. You do not lose any existing SEO authority. That is a guarantee.
