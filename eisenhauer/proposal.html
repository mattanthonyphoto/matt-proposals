Audit Findings

Three findings from the data that matter most.

1. Munter owns the searches that should be yours

On the keyword set a luxury East Kootenay buyer actually types — custom home builder Kimberly, luxury homes Cranbrook, timber frame builder Kootenays, passive house Kootenay, architect-led home builder Kimberly — Munter ranks in the top 3 on the majority. You rank outside the top 10 on all of them, and on several you don't appear on page 1 at all.

This isn't because Munter builds better houses. It's because their site is structured for search, and yours isn't.

2. The website isn't telling Google (or ChatGPT) where you build

There's no location-specific service page, no dedicated "Kimberly custom home builder" page, no "East Kootenay luxury builder" page, no city-keyed content. Search engines can't rank you for searches you haven't told them you serve. Neither can the generative tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) that are increasingly where high-net-worth buyers start their research.

3. Your content velocity is zero

Your blog hasn't published in 18+ months. Your "In Progress" social series stopped 149 weeks ago. You have no LinkedIn presence at all. For a builder of your caliber, your digital footprint should be one of your biggest sales assets — right now it's a liability.

Priority 1 — Fix the conversion (Months 1–2)

Priority 2 — Beat Munter (Months 2–6)

Now we go after the rankings.

  • Keyword + GEO strategy. Target the 20 highest-intent terms in your market. Publish content that claims each one.
  • Two long-form blog posts per month, written in your voice using the brand-voice extraction system, optimized for both Google and generative search.
  • Monthly new page publishing — each month adds a new service page, location page, or pillar post. Twelve months from now, your site has 24+ new indexed pages targeting Munter's keyword set.
  • Rank tracking + monthly reporting. You see exactly where you rank against Munter week-over-week.

Priority 3 — Build the social + LinkedIn machine (Months 3–6)

Once the foundation holds, we turn on distribution.

  • LinkedIn launch. Company page, thought-leadership content for you, project features, behind-the-scenes. Your buyers are on LinkedIn. Your competitors aren't using it well. Open lane.
  • Instagram revival. "In Progress" series back on. Stories tied to active projects. Highlights properly managed. Content pillars defined.
  • Local photographer brief (optional). I provide a shot list tied to content pillars. You source a Kootenay-based photographer for fill content between my quarterly trips. Keeps the feed alive.

Priority 4 — Awards + publications (Months 4–12)

Priority 5 — Lead tracking (Month 1, then ongoing)

You said it yourself: you don't know where leads come from. We fix that on day one.

  • UTM parameter setup on every outbound link (social, email, LinkedIn, ads).
  • GA4 + Search Console properly wired to the new site.

  • Monthly report: leads by source, cost per lead (on anything paid), conversion rates by channel.

Three months in, you can tell me with precision which channels are working and which aren't. That's the ROI clarity you've been missing.

The Tamarack Transition

Zero downtime. Guaranteed.

You flagged you're in a contract with Tamarack and don't know who owns your domain. Here's how I handle that.

  • Before anything else: I provide you a one-page checklist — who to call, what to request, what documents you need from Tamarack. You forward it to Justina or to Tamarack directly.
  • Parallel build. I build the new site on a staging environment that doesn't touch your live site. Nothing changes for your current visitors while we build.
  • Contract timing. Tell me when Tamarack ends. I time launch to the day after. No overlap in fees.
  • Cutover in one afternoon. DNS change, SSL verification, analytics preservation, 301 redirects for any URL that's changing. Site is never offline more than a few minutes.
  • Handoff protocol. If Tamarack owns any assets (domain, image files, account logins), we retrieve them before launch. I have a script for this conversation if you want one.

You do not lose a day of visibility during the transition. You do not lose any existing SEO authority. That is a guarantee.
