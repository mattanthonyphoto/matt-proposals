The Approach

How Material
Meets Light

Landscape architecture is one of the most difficult disciplines to photograph well. The work exists in time, weather, and season. A single frame has to convey what unfolds across years.

I approach each project as a study of the relationships your team designs: stone against water, steel against sky, planted form against built edge. The camera captures one moment, but the image has to communicate the full spatial and material experience.

The difference between a photograph that documents a project and one that advances a practice is design literacy. I don't just see the garden. I see the grading, the sight lines, the material palette, the way the planting softens the architecture.

The Process

From Conversation
to Final Delivery

Every engagement starts with understanding the design intent behind the work. From there, the process is straightforward.

01
Discovery

A 20-minute call to understand the project, the design narrative, the intended audience, and how the images will be used. I review drawings, renderings, and any published material to arrive prepared.

02
The Shoot

On site for a full day, sometimes two. I work around natural light cycles: dawn for soft volumes, midday for material contrast, twilight for atmosphere. Drone coverage is included where the site warrants it.

03
Delivery

A curated set of 25-40 publication-ready images delivered via Dropbox within 10 business days. Full commercial usage rights. Every image edited to a consistent editorial standard.

For landscape architecture specifically: I schedule shoots to capture planting at peak season and hardscape in optimal light. If a project benefits from seasonal documentation, I'll return across multiple visits at no additional charge for the Creative Partner tier.
