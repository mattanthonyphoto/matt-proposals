How a shoot day works

Three hours, no scripts

The whole point: you keep working. We capture what's already happening — the sound, the bench, the details — and turn that into the next month of content.

Before

What you wear

  • Whatever you'd wear on a normal Tuesday
  • Shop apron is a yes
  • No glamour shots, no styling — that reads false
  • We bring the lapel mic and clip it on when you arrive
Hour 1

Shop walk + audio

  • You take Matt around the bench
  • Talk through what's on each station — like you'd brief a journeyman
  • Lapel mic catches everything
  • That's the entire founder voice ask. Done in an hour
Hour 2

Stills + macro

  • Matt shoots details — joinery, grain, hardware, finish
  • You go back to whatever was on the bench
  • No interruption needed
  • 50+ stills banked here
Hour 3

B-roll + finish

  • Re-saw running, vacuum press, finishing room
  • Install crew shots if a truck is loading
  • 20 min B-roll for reels
  • We're out by lunch

What we leave with: 50+ stills, 20 min of B-roll, 30+ min of natural shop audio. What you give up: roughly 60 focused minutes, mostly walking the floor talking like you already do. The other two hours, you're working.

Format Playbook

How each format works

Click any to expand. Built from current 2026 data — not guesswork.

▶

Reels

4–5 / month · 30–60 sec
  • Length: 30–60 sec default. Hook in first 3 sec.
  • Formula: Process B-roll with your bench audio over the top. Captured live during shoot day — no scripts, no camera-direct. Voice-led. Face on camera optional later, when it feels right.
  • Hook example: "Three slices from one log. Same grain, four doors."
  • Caption: 80–150 chars. Front-loaded. One material, one technique, one credit.
⬛

Carousels

5–6 / month · 8–10 slides
  • Slides: 8–10. One idea per carousel — no grab-bags.
  • Slide 1: cover with text — promise the lesson. Slide 2: deliver the hook.
  • Final slide: CTA — "save this," "DM us," or tag the design partner.
  • Caption: 150–600 words. Material specificity, design partner credit, technical narrative.
  • Why this matters: 10× the engagement of single images in 2026.
▢

Single images

5–6 / month · macro / hero
  • Use: connective tissue. Not the strategy.
  • Subject: macro detail, finished hero, Friday shop scene.
  • Caption: 80–150 chars. One material, one technique, one credit.
in

LinkedIn

4–5 / month · Rick's profile
  • Where: Rick's personal profile, not the company page (5× the reach).
  • Who writes: we draft from your voice and your existing quotes (we have eight from your Woodworking Canada interview). You read and approve before anything posts.
  • Format: 200–400 word posts. Native video optional — audio-led, shot from the bench, no studio setup.
  • Frequency: 1/week floor, 2/week ceiling.

The Research

Why this approach

The plan above isn't built on instinct. It's built on what the field is actually doing, and what's actually working in 2026.

The numbers shaping the plan

10×
Carousels beat single images on engagement (10.15% vs 0.55%, SocialInsider 2026).
5×
Personal LinkedIn beats company page on reach for the same content (Refine Labs 2026).
5
Hashtag cap. Meta enforced this Dec 2025. 10+ now reduces reach.
70/30
Reel formula. Founder face/voice 70%, process B-roll 30%. Voiceover-over-process is dominant.

References we studied

The local field

We studied eight peer luxury millwork shops — local (Soma, Pacific Rim, Uppercase, Ingrain) and North American (Henrybuilt, Lanserring, Lancaster Customs, BH Woodturning). Local shops post 2–4 times a week, mostly single hero shots with short captions and inconsistent designer tagging. None are using founder voice. None are running material-education series. The field is wide open.
