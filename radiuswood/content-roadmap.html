The thinking behind this

We didn't write this from instinct. We benchmarked the field, mapped the architects in your service area, and pulled current 2026 format data for craft trades on Instagram and LinkedIn. The plan reflects what actually moves the needle, not what's traditional.

What the field is doing

We studied eight peer luxury millwork shops — three local (Soma, Pacific Rim, Uppercase, Ingrain) and five North American benchmarks (Henrybuilt in Seattle, Lanserring in NY/UK/Vienna, Lancaster Customs in Toronto, BH Woodturning, Daintree). Local shops post 2–4 times a week, mostly single hero shots of finished kitchens with short captions and inconsistent designer tagging. None are using founder voice. None are running material-education series. None are doing process carousels. The field is wide open.

Reference · Seattle
Henrybuilt

~10K followers. Documented case study (Shorty Awards): every project post credits the architect, designer, and builder by handle. That single discipline is what their Shorty case study calls out as the reason for repeat-business growth. The playbook for tagging.

Reference · USA
Blacktail Studio

408K Instagram, 2.68M YouTube. Built the entire account on a single reel formula: 30–90 seconds, voiceover over tight process cuts, a "stakes" hook in the first three seconds. This is the format we want Rick fluent in.

Format mix (revised from research)

Roughly 14–16 posts per month: ~40% carousel, ~25% reel, ~35% single image. This is a deliberate shift away from single-image-heavy posting — the 2026 data is decisive on carousels and reels carrying the work. One 20-minute strategy call each month to review what landed.

The five pillars

The Craft
30% — material education spine

Material and joinery close-ups, taught. Slip-matched veneer runs, book-matched panels, mitre details, finish layers. Every post names the species and the technique. This pillar carries the educational carousels — the highest-saving format on the calendar.

The Finished
25% — amplification engine

Hero shots and walk-through carousels of completed work. Every post tags the architect, designer, and builder by handle. This is where reach compounds when design partners re-share.

The Team
20% — founder voice (white space)

Rick on LinkedIn, in Rick's voice. The BCIT story, the one-car garage, AWMAC Gold, opinions on industry shifts. Native LinkedIn video for short clips. No local peer is doing this — it's the most defensible position in the plan.

The Shop
15% — the always-on engine

The 5,000 sq ft floor — re-saw, finishing room, milling, the no-CNC bench. Reusable assets that carry the calendar when installs are blocked. Daily rotation through stories.

The Install
10% — opportunistic

On-site, real-time. Site-measuring, scribe-fitting, the first piece in. Reel-and-stories first when access is short notice; full feed treatment when we get a half-day on site.

The format playbook

Each format has a specific recipe. We're not reinventing this — we're matching the templates that are demonstrably winning right now.

Reels (4–5/month)

  • Length: 15–30 sec for reach, 30–90 sec for saves and DM sends. Default to 30–60 sec.
  • Hook: the first three seconds carry it. State the stakes. "Three slices from one log. Same grain, four doors."
  • Structure: Rick on camera (face + voice) for the lead and the close, process B-roll cut underneath the voiceover for the middle. 70/30 founder-to-process.
  • Captions: front-load the first 125 characters with the hook. Keep total caption short — 80–150 characters.

Carousels (5–6/month)

  • Slides: 8–10. One idea per carousel — single-project walk-through, single-technique teardown, single-material lesson. No grab-bags.
  • Slide 1: a strong cover with text — promise the lesson. Slide 2: deliver the hook. Final slide: CTA — "save this," "DM us for the species," or tag the design partner.
  • Captions: medium-to-long (150–600 words). Material specificity, design partner credits, technical narrative.

Single images (5–6/month)

  • Use: connective tissue, not the strategy. Macro detail shots, finished hero stills, Friday shop scene.
  • Captions: short (80–150 char). Front-loaded. One material name, one technique, one credit.

LinkedIn (4–5/month, Rick's personal profile)

  • Format: 200–400 word founder posts, occasional native video (under 30 sec). Carousel PDFs for project case studies.
  • Voice: Rick. Lessons from 25 years on the bench, opinions on industry shifts, project-detail teardowns. Every post tags the design partner where applicable.
  • Frequency: 1/week is the floor; 3–4/week is the algorithm ceiling before reach decays. Start at 1, scale to 2.

Hashtags & captions

  • Five hashtags maximum — Meta capped this December 2025; 10+ now reduces reach. Mix two niche, two regional, one brand.
  • Keyword-rich captions outperform hashtag stacks by ~30% on reach in 2026 (Later). Write captions that name species, techniques, and design partners explicitly.

What we won't do

Just as important as what we shoot is what we don't. The 2026 data is clear on formats that worked in 2024 but no longer carry their weight.

  • Silent music-only B-roll reels. Watch completion lags voiceover by a wide margin. We'll script narration before every shoot day.
  • Aesthetic-only carousels. Pretty photos with no narrative collapsed in 2026. Every carousel teaches one thing.
  • Hashtag stacks of 20–30. Actively penalized now. Five tops.
  • Generic motivational LinkedIn posts. Engagement floor since LinkedIn's 2026 "value over engagement" shift.
  • Trend-chasing reels (transitions, dance audio). Never landed for craft niches.
  • Archive content from past Radius projects unless you whitelist a specific project. Photo rights and stale collaborator relationships make this risky.
  • Company-page-only LinkedIn posts. Native to your personal profile or it costs us 5× reach.

The three shoots

One half-day shoot per month, two to three hours on the floor. Each one is briefed against the format playbook above — Rick on camera with audio, B-roll for voiceover cuts, macro details for carousels, finished hero stills.

Shoot 01 · May · Shop Overview + Founder On Camera

The shop, the bench, and Rick on camera

This is the foundation shoot. Most important brief change from the playbook: we capture you on camera with audio. Three to five short voiceover takes (30–90 sec each) on the techniques you'll narrate over B-roll: re-saw, slip-matched veneer, the no-CNC bench, the finishing schedule, the AWMAC Gold lesson. Then we shoot the B-roll — re-saw running, vacuum press, finishing room, install crew loading the truck, hands at the bench. Together those bank the next four to six weeks of reels.

Plus the basics: 50+ stills (macro details, shop scenes, founder portraits), 20 minutes of B-roll, finished-piece hero shots if any are crated and ready to ship.

Proposed dates: Tuesday May 5, Wednesday May 6, or Thursday May 7. Pick the day the bench is at full pace. Half-day, 2–3 hours, no shutdown required. We'll send a 1-page brief 48 hours prior with the voiceover prompts.
Shoot 02 · June · Install or Finishing Room

On-site, or a deep look at finishing

Flexible by design. If a current install lands in the right window, we shoot on-site (templating, scribe-fitting, the first piece going in — gold for reels). If site access doesn't line up, we go deep on the finishing room — sample boards, sheen tests, the craft of a hand-rubbed finish, finisher portraits. Either way, more on-camera Rick takes for the August content runway.

Proposed window: Week of June 8–12. We'll lock the date one week prior, once the install schedule is clear.
Shoot 03 · July · Architect Partner Site Visit

Co-create with an active design partner

The amplification shoot. Pick one architect or designer we're currently building for, and shoot a moment together — site visit, drawing review, a piece going in. The output: a carousel that walks the architect's drawing → shop drawing → finished piece, plus a reel with both your voice and theirs. They tag, you tag, the work shows up in front of the right audience.

Proposed window: Week of July 13–17. Architect to be selected on the June strategy call from the priority list above.
