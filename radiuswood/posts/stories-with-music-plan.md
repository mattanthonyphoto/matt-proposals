# Radius — Stories with Music · June 2026 Plan

10 Instagram Stories drafted, each with a music direction. Stories drop alongside feed posts (1–2/day, dripped across the month). Audio is the discovery layer — IG boosts stories using trending audio.

## Music palette for Rick's brand

Match audio to brand voice: warm, considered, no corporate energy. Three lanes:

- **Bench sound** (raw, recorded on shoot day) — mallet taps, plane shavings, dust collection hum. Best for joinery/process stories.
- **Acoustic instrumental** (Pixabay / Epidemic Sound) — solo guitar, finger-picked. Warm, unhurried. Best for portrait / heritage / origin stories.
- **Light jazz piano / cinematic minimal** — sparse, considered. Best for award + retrospective stories.

Avoid: pop, trending dance audio, corporate stock music, anything with vocals (distracts from material content).

## Audio source recommendations

For Late.ai upload, each story attaches an audio file. Options:

1. **Bench sounds:** record live on next shoot day (free, brand-authentic).
   - 10-15 second clips of mallet tap, plane pass, spray gun, sander hum.
2. **Acoustic instrumental:** Pixabay free royalty-free.
   - Search: "Acoustic guitar slow" / "Finger-picked folk" — pull 5 candidates.
3. **Jazz piano / minimal:** Epidemic Sound (if subscribed) or Pixabay.
   - Search: "Slow piano minimal" / "Considered jazz piano".

## Trending IG audio (alternate path)

IG boosts stories that use **currently-trending audio** in the algorithm. Trade-off: you give up brand fit, you get reach.

For Radius's audience (architects + designers + luxury-home owners), trending pop/dance audio is OFF-brand. So stay on the curated audio path — discoverability comes from hashtags + ICP-targeted content, not algorithmic trending audio.
