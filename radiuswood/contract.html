2. Engagement Overview

The Service Provider is engaged as Radius Architectural Millwork's Social & Content Partner — a monthly visual and social retainer combining in-shop and on-site photography with ongoing social media management across Instagram and LinkedIn. The scope, pricing, and structure of this engagement are governed by the Creative Partner proposal (dated April 2026) and the Six-Month Plan delivered to Rick on the same date, with this Agreement formalizing the "Social & Content" tier Rick has elected to move forward with.

Structure:

  • 3-month minimum retainer at $1,500.00 CAD/month + GST, beginning May 1, 2026 and running through July 31, 2026
  • After the initial 3-month term, the engagement continues month-to-month, with either Party able to terminate on 30 days' written notice
  • An optional Creative Partner upgrade path is available at Month 3 if Rick elects to add website rebuild, SEO, AWMAC/Georgie submission support, and blog content (scoped separately at Creative Partner tier rates)

3. Scope of Services

3.1   Monthly Shoot Day

1 shoot day per month — up to 2–3 hours on site, across the 3-month term (and each month thereafter while the retainer is active).

Each shoot may be elected, month by month, as any one of:

  • Shop capture — Burnaby shop: machine room, finishing booth, material bay, team at work, panels on the bench
  • Install capture — jobsite photography during an active install window
  • Completion capture — finished project walkthrough when access and timing align
  • People-focused day — founder (Rick) and team profiles, behind-the-scenes, LinkedIn-first content
Each shoot day produces
  • 15–20 fully edited high-resolution images, delivered within 7 business days
  • 30–60 seconds of B-roll / short-form motion delivered alongside the stills
  • Multi-format delivery: web JPEG (sRGB, 2500px) + print JPEG (300 DPI) where required
  • Full perpetual commercial license for web, social, print, and submissions (per Section 6)

Shoot Rollover Provision. If a shoot cannot proceed in a given month due to weather, access, or scheduling, the shoot day rolls forward one month only. Unused rollover credits do not expire during the active term but cannot be carried beyond the end of the engagement.

3.2   Social Media Management

Ongoing social media content management across Instagram and LinkedIn:

  • 3–4 posts per week across Instagram and LinkedIn (~14–16 published posts per month)
  • Sourced from four pools: (i) the monthly shoot day, (ii) previous months' deliverables, (iii) existing Radius archive imagery that meets quality standards, (iv) archive remixes (carousels, before/after, material spotlights)
  • Caption writing, hashtag strategy, and a monthly posting calendar delivered in advance
  • Content pillar framework (5 pillars: The Craft, The Shop, The Install, The Team, The Finished) established in Month 1 and refreshed monthly
  • Story frames (5–8/month) for Instagram stories: behind-the-scenes, polls, reposts
  • Review link shared with Rick ahead of each week's posts for quick sign-off

Note: Day-to-day community management, DMs, and direct replies remain with Radius. The Service Provider supplies the assets, captions, and cadence; the Client posts and engages.

3.3   Monthly Strategy Check-In

  • One 20-minute strategy call per month reviewing what landed, what didn't, and what to shoot next
  • Pillar adjustments, content calendar for the following month, and install/completion scheduling handled on these calls

8. Cancellation & Termination

8.1   During the Initial 3-Month Term

The Client commits to the full 3-month retainer term and is responsible for all three monthly payments as scheduled in Section 4.1.

  • Client cancellation during the initial term: The Client may cease day-to-day services with 30 days' written notice but remains responsible for all three monthly retainer payments as originally scheduled. Already-paid amounts are non-refundable. The Client retains the perpetual license to all work delivered up to the cancellation date.
  • Service Provider termination during the initial term: The Service Provider may terminate with 60 days' written notice. Any unused prepaid retainer balance is refunded pro-rata.

8.2   After the Initial 3-Month Term

After July 31, 2026, the engagement continues month-to-month. Either Party may terminate with 30 days' written notice. The Client is responsible for any monthly invoices issued before the notice period expires.

8.3   Material Breach

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach that is not cured within 14 days of written notice specifying the breach. Material breaches include, without limitation: non-payment of invoices more than 45 days overdue, repeated failure to deliver within contracted turnarounds, or publication of imagery in violation of Section 6.

8.4   Pause Provision

With the Service Provider's written agreement (not unreasonably withheld), the Client may pause the retainer for up to 14 consecutive days once during the 3-month minimum. A pause extends the end date of the term by the same duration; it does not reduce the total number of monthly payments owed.

8.5   Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for failure to perform due to events beyond their reasonable control (natural disaster, epidemic, government order, extended loss of access to project sites). The affected Party will give prompt notice; timelines and deliverables will be adjusted by mutual agreement. If a force majeure event continues more than 60 consecutive days, either Party may terminate with written notice and a pro-rata refund of any unused prepaid amounts.

9. Site Access & Client Cooperation

The Client agrees to:

  • Provide access to the Burnaby shop during agreed shoot windows
  • Coordinate jobsite access for install or completion captures, including any homeowner / GC / designer permissions
  • Identify the focal areas, finishes, and craft decisions the Client wants captured
  • Respond to design sign-offs, caption approvals, and posting-calendar reviews within 3 business days so cadence is maintained
  • Nominate a single primary point of contact (Rick Wilson, unless otherwise agreed in writing) for approvals and scheduling

The Service Provider is not liable for missed posting windows or delivery delays caused by access issues, approval delays, or content sign-off delays outside the Service Provider's control.

The Client agrees to keep the Service Provider's pricing, methods, and proprietary processes confidential. These obligations survive termination of this Agreement.
