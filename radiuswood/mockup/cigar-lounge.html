The Brief

A room for a cigar collection. Five hundred square feet of slip-matched Eastern maple. One coffee table.

The brief was a quiet room in a home that's anything but quiet. Slip-matched Eastern maple cladding the full interior. A recessed television. A coffee table at the centre. Climate control underneath everything.

We started with the maple. Slip-matched panels mean the figure runs in continuous sequence across the room. We milled the stock ourselves, kept the run-of-grain in consecutive boards, and laid the panels up so the eye reads a single piece of timber across the long wall.

The coffee table sits in the centre. Slip-matched Eastern maple top, with an ebonized inlay running through its length. We didn't use real ebony. Real ebony comes from Africa and we don't bring it into the country. Instead we ebonized pear wood with an aniline dye, dark enough to read black, dimensionally stable, and sourced without controversy.

The finish is a water-based natural-effect topcoat. Historically the trade has used nitrocellulose lacquer for clear-over-light-wood work. Nitrocellulose ambers yellow with age — beautiful on walnut, less so on maple. The natural-effect product holds the colour of the raw wood while protecting the surface. Up to six coats, between-coat sanded, consistent sheen across the room.

Pull-out drawers under the coffee table for the smoking accessories. Tectus hinges on the cigar humidor. Recessed touch-latch on the door panels so nothing breaks the plane of the wall.

Material + Method

Five things on the bench for this one.

01
Slip-Matched Eastern Maple

Run-of-grain milled in sequence so the figure flows continuously across the long wall. Panels laid up consecutively, no jumps.

02
Ebonized Pear-Wood Inlay

Pear ebonized with aniline dye, used in place of real ebony for ethical and supply-chain reasons. Dark, stable, beautiful on the maple.

03
Natural-Effect Topcoat

Water-based, six coats, between-coat sanded. No ambering of the maple. Consistent sheen on every panel face.

04
Recessed Television Box

Wall-paneling encapsulates the television edge. No bracket visible, no frame proud of the panel face. The TV reads as part of the wall.

05
Touch-Latch Hardware

Tectus hinges on cigar storage doors, recessed touch latches on the room's panel doors. No knobs. No pulls. The plane stays clean.
