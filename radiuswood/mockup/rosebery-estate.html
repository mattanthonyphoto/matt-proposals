The Brief

A residence built around laminated curves and coffered ceilings.

Rosebery Estate was the project that asked the most of the shop in twenty-five years. A private Vancouver residence designed in collaboration with Brad Lamoureux of Lamoureux Architects, with millwork integrated into nearly every room. Eighteen months of bench work. Fifteen craftspeople, full-time, on this one project.

Alder was the primary species across the residence: doors, panelling, built-ins, the integrated case-good runs. Walnut was used for furniture pieces, lifted out of the wall plane to read as objects. The architect's drawing set ran six pages of finish schedule. We worked from his drawings; the shop drawings ran longer.

The project's signature challenge was the laminated curves. Bent-lamination of large millwork sections is a process where layers of veneer are glued together over a curved form, then released. The curve doesn't always hold the shape you laminated — it springs back. The amount it springs depends on species, thickness, glue chemistry, ambient humidity. We had to test, and re-test, and document the spring-back coefficient before we could commit to the radii on the architect's plans.

Coffered ceilings ran across the primary entertaining spaces. Every coffer was a joinery exercise: each rib mitre-cut to three sides, each panel scribed to the ceiling plane. We mocked up two complete coffers in the shop before site install began.

At the 2011 AWMAC BC Awards of Excellence, Rosebery won Best in Quality and Best in Service for the Large Residential category. Both gold medals on the same project.

Material + Method

What the bench actually built.

01

Laminated Curves

Bent-lamination of structural millwork radii. Spring-back testing across multiple stock thicknesses and humidity ranges before commit. Now down to a science.

02

Coffered Ceilings

Mitre-joined ribs scribed to the ceiling plane. Two full coffers mocked up in the shop before any site install.

03

Alder Primary

Solid stock and veneer-laid alder throughout. Doors, panelling, built-ins, case-good runs. The architect's primary species spec.

04

Walnut Furniture

Walnut lifted out of the wall plane to read as furniture, not architectural. Single-piece dining table and feature cabinets.
