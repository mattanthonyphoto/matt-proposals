How We Work

Six steps from drawing to install.

The same process for a single coffee table or eighteen months of integrated estate work. What changes is how many full-scale mock-ups we build before we cut.

01

Design

We work from your drawings or develop the millwork strategy from scratch. CAD shop drawings, 3D renders where they help.

02

Planning

Lead times, species sourcing, mock-up scheduling. The schedule we ship from. The schedule we hold to.

03

Production

Twenty craftspeople. Solid stock and veneer-laid work. Traditional joinery throughout. Zero CNC.

04

Mock-up

On novel joinery, we build a full-scale piece first. The client visits the shop and sees it at scale before we commit.

05

Finishing

Up to six coats of conversion varnish, between-coat sanded. Finish schedules to two pages where they need to be.

06

Installation

In-house installers. Site-measure, template, scribe-fit, punch list. We don't leave until it's flush.

Rick · At the bench · 2026
Founder

Twenty-five years on the bench.

Rick Wilson trained at BCIT and apprenticed under a Scandinavian master cabinetmaker before opening Radius in 2000. The shop has grown slowly. The work has not slowed down.

"We grew to twenty-three people once. The work didn't get better. My sleep got worse. So we trimmed back, narrowed who we work for, and got more particular about what leaves the shop. The phone has been busier ever since."

Today the shop runs at twenty craftspeople, half of them European-trained, in five thousand square feet of bench space. Sheldon, who runs the drawing desk, has been at Rick's side for eighteen years.

— Rick Wilson, Master Joiner · Owner
