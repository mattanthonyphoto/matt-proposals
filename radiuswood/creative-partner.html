The Problem

The builder schedules the shoot.
Your work becomes background.

Radius builds some of the finest architectural millwork in the Lower Mainland. AWMAC Gold. Bosa Properties penthouses. Lion's Bay estates. West Vancouver luxury homes. The craftsmanship speaks for itself on-site. But the photos rarely show it.

The architect or the builder books a photographer when the project is done. By then, the space is furnished, styled, and shot to make the overall design look good. Your slip-matched oak panel becomes wallpaper. Your mitered walnut corner becomes a backdrop for someone's sofa. The images you get back are usable, but they don't tell the story of the millwork. And once that window closes, it's gone — you can't go back and reshoot a kitchen island after a family has moved in.

That's why Radius has 262 images on the website and almost nothing on social media. You're waiting for someone else's photoshoot to get images of your own work, and by the time it happens, the moment to capture the millwork on its own has already passed.

The Solution

Shoot when the millwork
is the star.

Instead of waiting for the builder's photographer, we schedule around your installation timeline. Two types of content, two purposes.

Process & Installation Shots

  • 1-2 hours on-site while your team is installing
  • Captures the craftsmanship in action: fitting, leveling, finishing
  • Detail close-ups: grain, joinery, hardware, materials
  • Team at work: the people behind the product
  • Feeds social media with real, regular content
  • Tells the story of how the work gets done, not just the result

Millwork Completion Shoots

  • Scheduled when cabinets and millwork are installed, before furnishings
  • Your panels, your finishes, your precision are the entire frame
  • The images no one else on the project will have
  • Portfolio-grade photography for your website and awards
  • AWMAC and Georgie submission-ready from day one
  • These are your images, not the builder's outtakes

Why This Works for Millwork

Process shots give you consistent social media content every month without waiting for a finished project. Completion shoots give you portfolio images that actually showcase the millwork before the designer fills the room. Together, they solve the two problems you've had: no regular content, and no images that isolate your work.
