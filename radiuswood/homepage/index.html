The Pipeline

From shop drawings to install.

A project comes in as a set of drawings from the architect or designer. We bring it onto our drawing desk, work through the panel sequencing and the joinery details, and the saws don't move until that set is right.

  1. 01

    Read the set

    Architect or designer hands over plans, elevations, finish schedule, material spec. We mark the gaps and the assumptions, and come back with anything that needs to be locked.

  2. 02

    Shop drawings

    Every panel, every reveal, every section detail drawn before it gets cut. Forty-three sheets on a recent Whistler residence. The drawing is the contract.

  3. 03

    Bench production

    Table saw, jointer, shaper, edge bander, re-saw. Twenty craftspeople, half European-trained. Zero CNC — every mitre and reveal bench-cut the way the drawing called for.

  4. 04

    Finishing room

    Conversion varnish, six coats, between-coat sanded, climate-stable booth. Above ninety sheen the surface shows what was on the air four coats ago — dust is the enemy.

  5. 05

    Install support

    The package lands complete on site. Drawings, cuts, finishing, install support — one shop, one phone number. The install crew shouldn't be calling at eleven at night.

The Shop

Burnaby. Twenty-five years.

8575 Government Street. Five thousand square feet, purpose-built. Same view out the back since 2002. The neighbourhood became a trades district — when something needs a finisher or a metalworker we don't have time for, the next bay over has time.

Traditional joinery throughout — table saws, jointers, shapers, a wide-belt sander, a re-saw, a German-made edge bander. Same tools you'd find in any millwork shop two centuries ago. The dust collection is the only thing that's actually changed.

Twenty people on the bench. Half European-trained from Wilms and similar shops. The other half came through BCIT and apprenticed here. Average tenure: ten years. We hire for stubborn.
