The Shop, in plain terms

A Master Joiner-led shop in Burnaby.

Radius Architectural Millwork is a custom millwork shop founded in 2002 by Rick Wilson, BCIT Joinery Diploma (top of class, 1995) and four-year Wilms apprentice under a Scandinavian master cabinetmaker. The shop occupies 5,000 sq ft at 8575 Government Street, Burnaby BC, and has served residential architects, designers, and luxury homeowners across the Lower Mainland and Sea-to-Sky since.

We are zero-CNC by choice. Every mitre, every reveal, every joint is bench-cut on traditional tools — table saws, jointers, shapers, a wide-belt sander, a re-saw, a German edge bander. Drawings, cuts, finishing, install support — all under one roof.

The shop's hero credential is AWMAC BC Gold in 2011 for both Best in Quality and Best in Service on a Vancouver residence with Lamoureux Architects. We're documenting our current Whistler residence for the AWMAC 2027 submission.

The pipeline

From shop drawings to install.

  1. 01

    Read the set

    Architect or designer hands over plans, elevations, finish schedule, material spec. We mark gaps and assumptions, come back with anything that needs to be locked before the saws move.

  2. 02

    Shop drawings

    Every panel, every reveal, every section detail drawn before it's cut. Forty-three sheets on a recent Whistler residence. The drawing is the contract; the bench has the discretion.

  3. 03

    Bench production

    Table saw, jointer, shaper, edge bander, re-saw. Twenty craftspeople, half European-trained. Zero CNC — every mitre and reveal bench-cut the way the drawing called for.

  4. 04

    Finishing room

    Conversion varnish on a cup gun. Six coats, between-coat sanded, twenty-four-hour cure in a climate-stable booth. Above ninety sheen the surface shows what was on the air four coats ago.

  5. 05

    Install support

    The package lands complete on site. Drawings, cuts, finishing, install support — one shop, one phone number. The install crew shouldn't be calling at eleven at night.

The shop · Burnaby

Same building. Same view.
The trees got taller.

8575 Government Street since 2002. Five thousand square feet, purpose-built. Same view out the back since the day we signed the lease. The neighbourhood became a trades district — when something needs a finisher or a metalworker we don't have time for, the next bay over has time.

Traditional joinery throughout — table saws, jointers, shapers, a wide-belt sander, a re-saw, a German-made edge bander. Same tools you'd find in any millwork shop two centuries ago. The German edge bander is the most modern thing on the floor; the dust collection is the only thing that's actually changed.

Twenty people on the bench. Half European-trained from Wilms and similar heritage shops. The other half came through BCIT and apprenticed here. Average tenure: ten years. We hire for stubborn.
