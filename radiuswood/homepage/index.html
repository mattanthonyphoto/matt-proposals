How We Work

Drawings to the bench. Then to the install.

The same process for a single coffee table or eighteen months of integrated estate work. What changes is how many full-scale mock-ups we build before we cut.

I · Design

Drawing set in hand.

From the architect's drawing set or developed in-house. CAD shop drawings, full-scale templates where the joinery calls for it.

II · Planning

Species, lead times, mock-ups.

Slabs sourced, sequenced, and matched before milling. On novel joinery, a mock-up gets built first. The client sees it before we commit.

III · Bench

Cut, joined, assembled.

Twenty craftspeople. Solid stock and veneer-laid work. Hand-cut dovetails, mortise-and-tenon, bent-laminated radii. Zero CNC by choice.

IV · Finish

Up to six coats. Between-coat sanded.

Conversion varnish, hand-rubbed oil, ammonia-cured fumed oak. Finish schedules to two pages where the spec needs them there.

V · Install

In-house installers. Punch-list closed.

Site-measure, template, scribe-fit, install. The same hands that built it set it in place. The punch list is closed on the same trip.

The Shop

Same building, same bench.

Founded in Burnaby in 2002 by Rick Wilson — BCIT-trained Master Joiner, top of his class, and a builder long before that. Twigs in his parents' yard. Tables in their basement. One-car garage. Five-thousand square feet on Government Street.

Twenty craftspeople. Half European-trained. Traditional joinery throughout. Drawing, cut, bench, finish, install — all under one roof.

The shop has built residential and estate millwork across British Columbia for two and a half decades. Houzz Influencer with more than ten-thousand saves. AWMAC Gold winner in 2011.
