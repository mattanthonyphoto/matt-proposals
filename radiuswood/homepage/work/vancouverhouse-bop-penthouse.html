Our Work · Vancouver House

Vancouverhouse BOP Penthouse

The Project

Bookmatched walnut, inside Bjarke Ingels' tower.

A penthouse inside Bjarke Ingels Group's Vancouver House — the twisting triangle tower at the south edge of downtown Vancouver. The interior had to live up to the shell without competing with it. The brief landed on a single material gesture: American walnut, bookmatched, run as both ceiling slats and full-height cabinetry, with black marble as the counter-note. The standout is a three-storey Bocci pendant installation in the central stairwell, visible from inside through a glass wall and from the street at dusk. The master ensuite carries the move further with a curved walnut slat wall wrapping the freestanding tub — each slat cut as its own ribbon and set against a bent-laminated spine on a steel substructure.
