Indian River — The Point

The Project

Slip-matched rift oak through a gable-end residence.

A modern gable-end residence on Indian Arm where the architectural move is a single tall window framing the harbor — and the millwork has to hold its own next to it. Slip-matched rift white oak through the public rooms, a river-rock chimney column rising past the slats, black wenge as the dark counter-note in the dining wall, and a tongue-and-groove stair worked in the same oak as the cabinetry so it reads as one continuous material gesture. The flitch is sequenced on the floor in shop order before it sees a press; the figure lands at the door breaks and the appliance returns. A painted moss-green island with a honed soapstone top is the counterpoint to all the oak. Photography by Andrew Latreille.
