Indian Arm Study

The Project

Cedar, stone, and a single curved desk.

A writer's cabin on Indian Arm. Cedar tongue-and-groove for the ceiling, walls, and the full-height built-in bookcase that lines one side of the room. A wall of locally collected stone as the counter-note — dry-stacked, no mortar joint visible from the room side. Where the cedar meets the stone, the joint is scribed to the rock — a hairline reveal, no caulk, no margin. A curved-edge desk slab cantilevers off the cedar wall with no visible support; the structure runs back into the wall. A globe-pendant cluster on a red-cable harness drops from the cedar ceiling. The leather Maralunga is the only piece of furniture in the room that isn't built in.
