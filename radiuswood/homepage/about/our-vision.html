The Vision

Quality and sophistication, continuously evolving.

To create an emotional experience and unique environments within the home — through physical form and texture in wood. The shop runs on traditional and contemporary joinery, engineered into the work where the eye won't see it but the hand will.

The attention to detail we put into every project is uncommon, even in the exclusive luxury millwork marketplace. We design every project to last generations, not seasons. Same shop, same bench, same Government Street address since the day we opened.

"The curve doesn't always hold the shape. We test, we measure, we log the spring-back per species and humidity. Eventually you have it down to a science."
Rick Wilson · Owner + Master Joiner
Principles

The five rules we hold the work to.

01

The figure runs the room.

Veneer gets slip-matched and sequenced in the order it came off the log. Every door, every panel, every drawer face from the same flitch. You stop seeing cabinetry and start seeing the tree.

02

Joinery engineered in.

Pin-and-tail dovetails. Mortise-and-tenon. Bent lamination over custom jigs. The joinery is structural and invisible — built into the work where the eye won't see it, but the hand will.

03

Zero CNC, by choice.

All cuts, all joinery, all assembly are bench work. The shop is set up for hand-craft because some details — a slip-matched veneer return, a hand-cut dovetail, a tested bent radius — can't be machine-cut and still hold up under raking light.

04

In-house, concept to install.

Drafting, mock-up, production, finishing, install, punch-list — all under one roof, all by the same team. No sub-contracted finishing. No sub-contracted install. The hands that build it are the hands that finish it.

05

Built for generations.

Conversion varnish, six coats, between-coat sanded. Solid-stock hardwood case goods. Substrate built for dimensional stability. The work is meant to outlast the trends that surrounded it.
