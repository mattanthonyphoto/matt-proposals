How We Collaborate

From the drawing set to the punch-list.

Most projects come in through architects and designers we have worked with before. We slot in at the drawing-review stage — early enough to flag dimensional issues in the millwork run, late enough that the design intent is locked. From there we develop the shop drawings, mock the critical assemblies, and run the project from bench to install.

The list below is selected — not exhaustive. We name the firms whose work we are allowed to credit publicly, and the photographers whose images live across this site.

Architects + Designers

Residential collaborations
  • Lamoureux Architects
    Rosebery Estate — Vancouver West. AWMAC BC Gold 2011 (Best in Quality + Best in Service). Bent-laminated alder rotunda, slip-matched panelling, walnut furniture. $1.5M millwork scope.
  • Shift Interiors
    Indian River — The Point — Indian Arm, North Vancouver. Modernist gable-end residence with slip-matched rift white oak interior, black wenge dining wall, river-rock chimney column, tongue-and-groove stair.
  • Inside Bjarke Ingels Group's Vancouver House
    Vancouverhouse BOP Penthouse — 1480 Howe Street. Full-house millwork inside BIG's twisting Vancouver House tower. Bookmatched walnut slat ceilings, three-storey Bocci pendant stairwell, curved walnut slat wall in the master ensuite.
