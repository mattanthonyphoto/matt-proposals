Overview

What "architectural millwork" covers, in practice.

Most of what we build falls under this category. Architectural cabinetry for kitchens, bathrooms, libraries, and offices. Wall panelling for public rooms — slip-matched walnut, rift oak, bookmatched anigre. Case goods and built-ins. Custom passage and entry doors.

The scope can be a single feature wall or the millwork package for a whole house — Indian River, Vancouverhouse, Waterfront and Lion's Bay were all full-house scopes. Whatever the project, the standard is the same: slip-matched veneer, hand-cut joinery, conversion-varnish finish, in-house install.

Process

Five phases through the shop.

Same team from drawing review to punch-list. We measure twice, we test the joint, we don't ship a piece until it's right.

01

Concept + Drawings

Architect's drawing set in. Shop-side detailing. Sub-trade coordination upfront.

02

Drafting + Mock-Up

3D renders to scale. Material samples. Full mock-ups of critical assemblies.

03

The Bench

Slip-matched veneers sequenced from the same flitch. Hand-cut joinery.

04

In-House Finishing

Conversion varnish to hard-wax oil. Six coats. Between-coat sanded.

05

Install + Punch-List

Shipped and installed in-house. Every punch-list item closed by the team that built it.
