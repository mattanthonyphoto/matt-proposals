Technique

The vocabulary, in practice.

01 · Matching

Slip · Sequence · Book

Veneer matching is the first decision on any panel run. We slip-match for a continuous grain story across a wall, sequence-match for door faces that read as one piece, book-match where the figure deserves a mirror.

slip-matched · sequence-matched · book-matched
02 · Joinery

Pin-and-tail · Mortise-and-tenon

Traditional joinery throughout. Pin-and-tail dovetail for every drawer box — no fasteners, the joint geometry is what holds it. Mortise-and-tenon for frame-and-panel. Bench-cut, by hand or on traditional jigs.

dovetail · mortise · tenon · zero CNC
03 · Finish

Conversion Varnish · 6 Coats

Six coats of conversion varnish on a cup gun. Between-coat sanded at 220. Climate-stable booth, 24-hour cure. Above 25-degree sheen we cut polish — gun-finish above 90 shows every flaw.

conversion varnish · between-coat sanded · cup gun

Frequently asked

About custom millwork.

01What scope of millwork project does Radius take on?
Most projects fall between $25,000 and $1.5M+ in millwork scope. We're set up for full-residence packages — kitchens, wardrobes, library walls, wine cellars, custom furniture, panelling — rather than one-off cabinets. The shop has fifteen craftspeople on average dedicated to a single major project at any time.
02Why is Radius zero-CNC?
Custom residential millwork is mostly one-offs. Setting up a CNC for a one-off radius is a day of programming and a day of test cuts. We can do the same curve on the band saw in two hours. CNC pays off in volume production — not in our work. Every mitre, every reveal, bench-cut, by hand or on traditional jigs. It's the difference you can see at forty feet.
03What's the typical timeline from drawings to install?
Depends on scope. A single-room cabinetry package is typically 8–12 weeks from approved drawings to install-ready. A full-residence package (kitchen + wardrobes + panelling + furniture) can run 12–24 months. AWMAC Gold Rosebery was eighteen months on the bench with fifteen craftspeople full-time.
04Do you work directly with the architect or through the GC?
Either, depending on the project. Most often we're engaged by the architect or designer at the construction-documents stage and stay on the project through install. We translate the architect's drawing set into shop drawings — every panel, every reveal, every section detail — and the contractor coordinates the install timeline.
05What materials and finishes do you specialise in?
Solid stock and veneered work in: alder (incl. bent lamination), walnut, rift & quartersawn white oak, fumed white oak, slip-matched and sequence-matched Eastern maple, ammonia-cured smoked oak, ebonised pear inlay (ethical alternative to African ebony), sapele, mahogany, and heritage species on request. Finishes: conversion varnish (default), water-borne where required, oil-and-wax on heritage pieces.
