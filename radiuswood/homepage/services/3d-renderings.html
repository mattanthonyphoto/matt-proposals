Services · 3D Renderings

See it built, before we cut.

Why Render

Precisely scaled measurements in real-life digital form.

Radius Architectural Millwork 3D renderings incorporate precisely scaled measurements that result in an accurate, real-life digital depiction of your project. They allow you to evaluate the design and make modifications before construction begins. For commercial projects they double as marketing assets; for residential projects they read as design inspiration before the bench picks up its tools.

  • Models from photos, drawings, designs, or charts. Whatever source material exists, the rendering brings it to scale.
  • Realistic example before construction starts. Planners view the project in full proportions — what works, what conflicts, what needs to move.
  • Avoid delays and minimize costly errors. Issues caught on screen don't surface on site. The render saves time and money on the build.

Two Things, Not One

3D rendering vs. 3D modeling — not the same thing.

Worth getting the terminology right because clients sometimes use the terms interchangeably. 3D rendering is taking a photo; 3D modeling is creating a physical replica. Both are useful, but they answer different questions.

3D Rendering

Digitalizes the model — produces photographs and visual analysis from a digital depiction. Use it when you need to see, compare, and decide. Ideal for design reviews, client presentations, finish selection, sub-trade coordination, and marketing assets.

3D Modeling

Creates a physical replica — a tactile prototype that can be examined and handled. Use it when the question is whether the assembly works in three dimensions, not just on screen. We pair modeling with the bench mock-ups in Phase 04 of our process.
