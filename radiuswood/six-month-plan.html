The Question

The concern isn't the math.
It's the source.

When you said you struggled to envision what content from a once-a-month shoot would look like, the real question underneath was: what are we even shooting? Your installs are slow. The Whistler project got locked out by the architect. Angus got pre-shot by a design firm. If the content engine depends on finished installs, it breaks every time someone else controls the site.

So the plan isn't built around installs. It's built around your shop in Burnaby, which you control every day of the week, plus installs and completions as they line up.

Four Sources

Where the content
actually comes from.

A typical month blends four sources of raw material. One shoot day is your monthly commitment — everything else is remixing, reframing, and writing around the visual library we build together.

Why The Shop Is The Unlock

Anatomy of One Shoot

What a single shoot day
actually produces.

One shoot day is 2-3 hours on site or in the shop. I capture around 150 frames, edit the strongest 15-20 into a finished set, and pull 30-60 seconds of motion along the way. Here's how that one day translates into a month of posts.

Input

One shoot day. 2-3 hours.
Shop floor, install site, or finished project. ~150 captured frames. 30-60 seconds of B-roll. One strategy briefing before, one delivery after.

The Six-Month Roadmap

What it looks like
month by month.

Each month has a theme, a shoot focus, and a milestone. The plan adjusts when real installs and completions land — OGR, Angus reshoot, and anything else that comes in gets slotted in opportunistically.

Baseline visual library. Establish voice and identity.
