Why this project

I don't pitch every build. I'm pitching this one.

23rd & Ontario is the kind of project I want in my portfolio — and the kind of project that deserves to be documented from the dirt up. Every milestone, every detail, every decision your crew makes that an outsider would never see.

By the time keys are handed over, you walk away with three things working for you, all built off the same set of site visits.

01

The Take-Home Book

A printed, hardcover documentation book — given to your client at handover. The kind of object they keep on the coffee table and show every guest who walks through the door.

02

The Sales Tool

A tangible artefact you hand to the next prospect on a discovery call. Nothing closes a high-end residential lead faster than a beautifully designed book of a build you finished last year.

03

The Content Library

Every site visit produces 10–20 images for Instagram, your website portfolio, and any future pitch materials. A year's worth of in-progress content, captured as you go.

One Engagement, Three Assets

Most builders get a portfolio shoot at the end and that's it. The build itself — the part your followers and prospects find most compelling — disappears into camera rolls and group chats.

This program captures the whole arc, then turns it into something physical you can hand to a client and something visible you can post for a year.

How it works

Five steps. No babysitting required.

02
