SEO & Site Audit

A luxury studio.
A starter-kit website.

I crawled goosendesign.com and pulled the sitemap, source, social handles, and press footprint. The studio's project list is genuinely impressive. The website that represents it is not.

What's Working
✓
Public Showroom
Camerich, Ethnicraft, and Keilhauer in Lower Gibsons. Most interior designers don't have this. It's a content asset that isn't being used.
✓
Real Press Coverage
Coast Reporter feature on Mad Hen, Beachcomber Coffee, Coastline Health, Blue Heron Inn. Sprudge mention on Beachcomber. You can earn coverage.
✓
Marquee Project List
Mad Hen, Blue Heron Inn, Coastline Health, Beachcomber Coffee, Balsam Way, restaurants for Chef Dale MacKay and Chef Darren MacLean.
✓
Permit-Drawing Qualified
Full-service capability beyond decoration. A real differentiator vs Sunshine Coast competitors.
What Needs Fixing
✗
Two Project Pages Still Named "Project Title 6" and "Renovation Project"
Wix template placeholders that were never renamed. Anyone clicking the portfolio sees them. This is the single most credibility-damaging item on the site.
✗
Major Projects Missing From Portfolio
Blue Heron Inn, Coastline Health, Balsam Way, and the Dale MacKay restaurants are absent from the website. Several of the biggest projects in the studio's history aren't represented online.
✗
Zero Photographer / Architect / Builder Credits
Not a single project page credits anyone. No photographer, no architect, no builder, no client. This kills referral SEO and signals that visual assets are not being managed professionally.
✗
No Project Narratives
Every project page is image-only. No design brief, no problem-solution story, no materials list, no specs, no client outcomes. Visitors leave without understanding what Goosen actually delivers.
✗
Project Pages Have No Meta Descriptions
Empty <meta description> on every project URL sampled. Search engines have nothing to display in results. Easy fix, never done.
!
Wix JavaScript Rendering
Site is rendered client-side via Wix Thunderbolt. Google handles it, but slowly. Mobile load is sluggish on LTE due to uncropped 24MP source images.
!
No Schema Markup
Zero structured data. No LocalBusiness, no InteriorDesigner, no Organization, no BreadcrumbList. Google can't classify what the site is.

SEO Bottom Line

The site isn't slightly underperforming. It's actively undermining a luxury positioning. Two placeholder project names, missing flagship work, zero credits, and no narrative. The fix isn't patching Wix. The fix is rebuilding around the projects that should have been on the homepage all along.

Local SEO & Discovery

609 followers.
17 connections.

Where can a homeowner or restaurateur on the Sunshine Coast actually find Goosen Design? Almost nowhere.

Social Channels
✗
Instagram @goosendesign — 609 Followers, 23 Posts
For a 10-year-old studio marketing itself as luxury, this is invisible. The follow:follower ratio (1:1.9) suggests follow-back grinding, not organic earned reach. Posting cadence is roughly 2 posts per year. Account is functionally dead.
✗
LinkedIn Company Page — 17 Followers
One employee listed (Benjamin). Most recent post March 2026. For a studio doing commercial and residential work across the Coast and the Lower Mainland, LinkedIn invisibility is leaving money on the table.
✗
Facebook Page — Effectively Dead
Page exists at facebook.com/110832615338217 but returns 404 / logged-out errors. Not surfacing in search. Unmaintained.
✗
No Pinterest Presence
Surprising and damaging. Pinterest is the #1 discovery platform for residential interior design. Every Sunshine Coast competitor has at least a basic Pinterest footprint.
✗
No Houzz Profile
Sunshine Coast interior design competitors (Landon Dix, Durston Design, Sunshine Coast Home Design, Carena Dean) all maintain Houzz profiles. Goosen has none. Significant lead-gen gap.
Reviews & Reputation
✗
Zero Public Reviews Anywhere
No reviews surfaced on Google, Houzz, Facebook, or any directory. For a studio claiming "international award-winning" status, this is a credibility hole the moment a buyer Googles you.
!
"International Award-Winning" Claim Unverified
The About page asserts international award-winning status. I checked ARIDO, Western Living Designers of the Year, Robert Allen, IIDA, and other major databases. No verifiable record. The claim appears self-attributed.

Local SEO Bottom Line

This isn't a marketing problem. It's a content problem. You don't post because you don't have shoots to post. You don't get reviews because you don't have a system to ask. You don't get featured because you don't have submission-grade imagery to submit. Every gap traces back to the same root cause.
