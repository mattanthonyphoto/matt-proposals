Where You Are

The portfolio is luxury.
The presentation isn't.

Ethan, I dug into Goosen Design before our call — the website, sitemap, all four social channels, your press footprint, and the project list referenced in Coast Reporter. Here's the honest read.

Your project list is the project list of a Western Living Designer of the Year nominee. Mad Hen. Blue Heron Inn. Coastline Health. Beachcomber Coffee. The Calgary Guardian Towers penthouse for Hon Developments. Restaurants for Chef Dale MacKay and Chef Darren MacLean. Twenty years of design experience and a public showroom in Lower Gibsons.

But the website tells a different story. Two project pages are still named "Project Title 6" and "Renovation Project" because the Wix template defaults were never renamed. Four of your most publishable projects — Blue Heron Inn, Coastline Health, the Calgary penthouse, and the Dale MacKay restaurants — aren't in the portfolio at all. Instagram has 609 followers and posts roughly twice a year. LinkedIn has 17 followers. There's no Houzz profile. There's no Pinterest. There are no photographer credits, no architect credits, no project narratives, and no published-in mentions anywhere on the site.

This isn't a marketing problem. It's a content problem. You don't post because you don't have shoots. You don't get featured because you don't have submission-grade imagery. The fix is putting a creative partner behind Goosen who captures every project to editorial standard, prepares it for publication, and feeds the channels you don't have time to feed yourself.
