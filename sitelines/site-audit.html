Local SEO & GEO Audit

Local visibility.
Can they find you?

When a developer in Langley or an institutional client in Abbotsford searches for an architect, Google serves results based on local signals: Google Business Profile, directory citations, and LocalBusiness schema. Site Lines is missing all three.

Google Business Profile
✗
Google Business Profile not detected
GBP is the single most important local ranking factor. Without it, Site Lines doesn't appear in Google Maps or the local pack. Every competitor with a GBP outranks you for local searches.
✗
No LocalBusiness schema markup
Add ArchitecturalFirm or LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema with NAP, hours, and service area. This feeds Google's Knowledge Panel directly.
Directory Presence
✓
Listed on Houzz
Houzz profile exists with 6 awards. Good baseline citation.
!
Not found on HomeStars
HomeStars is a high-authority Canadian directory. A profile here builds citation consistency.
!
NAP consistency needs verification
Name, address, and phone must match exactly across website footer, Houzz, and any other listings. Inconsistencies confuse Google.

What I'd Fix First

The action plan.
Priority order.

Site Lines has a strong foundation: 25 years of work, a clean website, and real project depth. But the digital infrastructure isn't working as hard as it should. Here's where to focus.

1

Add Structured Data + Fix Image SEO

Implement ArchitecturalFirm JSON-LD schema and add descriptive alt text to all 320 images. This unlocks Google Image Search and AI search engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity. Highest ROI fix on this list.

2

Claim Google Business Profile

Set up GBP with consistent NAP, project photos, and service areas. This is the only way to appear in Google Maps and the local pack when developers and institutional clients search for architects in the Fraser Valley.

3

Document Built Work at Award Level

Replace renderings with professional photography of completed projects. Shoot to award-submission specifications so every completed project becomes a potential AIBC, RAIC, or publication credential. After 25 years, the portfolio should be generating recognition, not just filling a gallery.
