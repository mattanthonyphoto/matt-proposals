Website Assessment

Your site is built on Squarespace 7.1 with minimal customization. It's clean and functional, but it looks like a default template — it could be any trades business. For a boutique architectural millwork shop doing high-end custom work in Whistler, the website should feel as intentional as your joinery.

The homepage hero is a single static image with a short tagline. No video, no motion, no visual impact. Project pages contain 1-2 sentences each with no story about materials, process, challenges, or scope. Your Contact page has only an email address — no form, no phone number, no map. There are no calls to action anywhere on the site.

Your Western Living feature and Home of the Year finalist status are mentioned once, buried on a single project page. There is one client testimonial on the entire site.

The Photography Gap

You clearly value professional photography — you credit photographers on every project. But you're using 7 different photographers across 8 portfolio projects. Ema Peter, Upper Left, Janis Nicolay, Darby Magill, Kyle Graham, Kristen McGaughey, Conrad Brown. Every project has a different visual language. There's no cohesive brand identity across the portfolio.

One photographer who understands your brand, your materials, and your process creates consistency that compounds over time — across your website, social media, awards submissions, and press pitches.

SEO & Technical Gaps

Critical
