Pillar 01

New leads, every week.
Without lifting a finger.

You told me you're doing all your outreach manually. One email at a time, when you have a spare moment between projects. That's not a lead generation system — it's a side task competing with your actual work. Here's what changes.

I build a research-backed prospect list of builders, architects, and developers in the Sea-to-Sky corridor and Greater Vancouver who don't have a go-to interior designer. Every contact is verified. Every email is personalized — referencing their recent projects, their build style, their market position. Not a blast. Not a template. A professional introduction from your domain, sent at scale.

The system runs a 3-step sequence: an introduction email, a follow-up three days later, and a graceful close four days after that. If someone replies, the sequence stops automatically. I triage every response, filter out the noise, and hand you the warm leads with context so you can have a real conversation.

What You're Doing Now

  • Manual emails, one at a time
  • No systematic follow-up
  • Time-consuming prospect research
  • Inconsistent volume (depends on your schedule)
  • No tracking or optimization
  • Growth capped by your available hours

What This System Does

  • 50-75 personalized emails per week, automated
  • 3-step sequences with intelligent follow-up
  • Verified prospect database, continuously refreshed
  • Consistent outreach whether you're shooting or designing
  • Monthly performance data (open rates, replies, leads)
  • Growth decoupled from your time

The Math

At 50-75 cold emails per week, you're reaching 200-300 qualified prospects per month. Industry response rates for personalized B2B outreach run 5-15%. Even at the conservative end, that's 10-15 warm conversations per month with builders and architects who know your name and have seen your work. If one in five of those conversations converts, that's 2-3 new projects per month entering your pipeline. All while you're designing.

Why Custom Code Matters

Template websites on Wix or Squarespace look fine. But they generate the same HTML structure as every other template site, which means Google can't distinguish your work from a DIY hobbyist. Custom code means proper schema markup (telling Google you're an interior design studio in Pemberton), optimized image loading (your portfolio loads fast on every device), and SEO structure that compounds over time. Six months in, your site is ranking for "interior designer Whistler" and "custom home design Pemberton" while your competitors are invisible.
