Website & SEO

Your website is your
24/7 sales team.

Kombi Construction's website at kombihomes.com has 2 pages. Here's what's working and where the biggest SEO wins are.

Your site doesn't have structured data markup (JSON-LD), which means Google can't pull your business details into rich search results. Adding LocalBusiness schema is the single fastest technical win available.

Dedicated project pages are where SEO and client trust intersect. Each completed build should have its own page with professional photos, specs, and location tags. These are what rank in Google.

Your sitemap is set up, which helps Google index your pages.

Quick Wins

Add structured data (JSON-LD)Helps Google understand your business. LocalBusiness + Organization schema at minimum.
Create project portfolio pagesEach completed project gets its own page with professional photos, specs, and client context.
Write location-specific pagesNorth Vancouver BC and surrounding areas. One page per service area.
Fix meta descriptionsEvery page needs a unique, compelling meta description under 160 characters.
Add a blog with 2 posts/monthProject case studies, build process insights, and local market content.
Optimize images with alt textEvery image needs descriptive alt text. Good for SEO and accessibility.

The Professional Photography Connection

Every recommendation above needs one thing to work: strong visual content. Project pages need professional photography. Blog posts need real images from real builds. Location pages need hero shots from that area. The website strategy and the content strategy are the same strategy.

Referral System

Turn happy clients into
your sales team.

Most of your best projects probably came from referrals. The problem is that referrals happen randomly. A system makes them predictable.

In North Vancouver BC, word of mouth is everything. But right now it's accidental. Someone mentions you at a dinner party, maybe the friend follows up, maybe they don't.

The builders consistently landing the best projects have three things in common: they ask, they make it easy, and they follow up.

1

The Ask

At project handover, ask directly: 'We loved working on this project. If you know anyone building or renovating, we'd appreciate the introduction.' Simple, honest, no incentive needed.

2

Make It Easy

Give them something to share. A link to the project page on your website with professional photos. People share beautiful things without being asked twice.

3

The Follow-Up

Thank every referrer personally. A handwritten note or a framed print of their home goes further than any gift card. Stay in their world with a quick check-in every 6 months.

The Photography Connection

The single best referral tool is a stunning project page with professional photography. When a past client tells their friend 'you should call our builder,' the friend Googles you. If what they find looks as good as the recommendation, you get the call. If it doesn't, you lose it.
