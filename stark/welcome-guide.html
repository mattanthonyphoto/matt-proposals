About

Documenting Design Intent

Based here in Squamish. I work with architects, builders, and interior designers across the Sea-to-Sky, Sunshine Coast, Vancouver, and the Okanagan. Transport Canada certified Advanced Drone Pilot. 19 projects documented across BC.

My focus is the relationship between built form and site — how high-performance envelopes meet their landscape, how material choices read in alpine light versus coastal flat light, how interiors connect to the views they were designed around. Not real estate photography. Design documentation that serves award submissions, publications, websites, and social media simultaneously.

Tranquil Retreat — Sandy Hook

Corrugated Steel Against Tidal Forest

Raw corrugated steel chosen to weather alongside the salt air and cedar canopy. The material palette — steel, cedar soffits, granite retaining walls — was selected to converge with the site over time, not resist it. Late afternoon light reveals every texture: the grain in the wood, the rivets in the steel, the moss already claiming the stone below.

The Perch — Sunshine Coast

Cantilevered Over Granite Shoreline

Charred cedar, blackened steel, floor-to-ceiling glass. The structure perches on a granite bluff above the Pacific — designed to vanish into the tree line from the water and reveal itself as a dramatic cantilever from the forest side. Two buildings, depending on where you stand.

One shoot generates five assets: award entries, publication pitches, website content, social media, and client presentations. The photography is done once. The leverage is ongoing.

Alpine Projects — Whistler

Snow Load, Dark Timber, Stone

Deep snow compresses the tonal range. Alpine light shifts hour to hour, season to season. These are Whistler projects — the same corridor you work in. The Warbler's blackened timber cladding absorbs light and recedes into the tree line by design. Autumn, when the surrounding larch turns gold, reveals the full silhouette without intervention.

Brown's Road — Roberts Creek

Board-Formed Concrete and Rain

A net-zero home designed to be read through its materials. Board-formed concrete carrying the grain of the formwork. Standing-seam metal channelling rainwater into planted swales. The performance is embedded in the material choices — not applied as a label. Flat coastal light reveals every ridge and imperfection, which is the entire point of choosing the material.

Salt Spring Island Cabin

Glass, Timber, and Island Light

A cabin scaled to its clearing. Oversized glazing panels frame the surrounding Garry oak and arbutus like a series of curated views — each window a deliberate composition. Early morning. Island light low and warm, catching the grain of exposed timber framing.

Peak Ventures — Whistler

Mountain Modernism

Clean lines against a complex landscape. The flat roofline and dark cladding create a deliberate horizontal plane that contrasts with the vertical thrust of the surrounding conifers. Dark stained cedar, blackened steel, stone. Each surface absorbs or reflects differently, giving the facade depth and weight that shifts with the light.

Gambier Island

Accessed Only by Water

No road access. Every beam, every fixture, every pane of glass was barged across Howe Sound and carried up the granite by hand. The architecture follows the natural clearing rather than imposing one, wrapping the existing rock face and preserving the forest canopy that gives the site its character.
