About

Based in Squamish

I'm an architectural and interiors photographer working across British Columbia — Vancouver and the Sea-to-Sky regularly, the Sunshine Coast, Vancouver Island, the Gulf Islands, and the Okanagan when the project asks for it. The work tends to centre on residential architecture and high-end interiors where the design choices were specific and deliberate. That's the photography I'm most interested in: imagery that communicates the why behind a room, not just its appearance.

Your studio's direction — clean lines, monochrome tones, a mix of modern contemporary with a vintage piece that anchors the room — reads as carefully edited. That kind of work doesn't photograph well under a rushed approach. It asks for patience: time to read how the vintage detail sits against the minimalist backdrop, how the tonal range of a monochrome palette resolves in camera, how a moment of warmth is placed inside an otherwise restrained composition. That's the kind of attention I bring to a shoot. Editorial standards come built in, since a meaningful share of my work ends up in publication or award submissions.

Working with firms including Sitelines Architecture, Balmoral Construction, Summerhill Fine Homes, LRD Studio, Shelter Residential Design, Michel Laflamme Architect, and The Window Merchant. Transport Canada certified Advanced Drone Pilot.

Selected Work

Recent Projects

A handful of pieces from across the province. The thread tying them together is the same one I'd bring to your work — pacing the imagery to read the way the design was actually meant to be experienced.

The Perch — Sunshine Coast

Cantilevered Over Granite Shoreline

Charred cedar, blackened steel, floor-to-ceiling glass. The structure perches on a granite bluff above the Pacific — designed to vanish into the tree line from the water and reveal itself as a dramatic cantilever from the forest side. Two buildings, depending on where you stand.

The Warbler — Whistler

Snow Load, Dark Timber, Stone

Deep snow compresses the tonal range. Alpine light shifts hour to hour, season to season. The Warbler's blackened timber cladding absorbs light and recedes into the tree line by design. Autumn, when the surrounding larch turns gold, reveals the full silhouette without intervention.

Fraser Valley Vista — Langley

Suburban Scale, Refined Detail

A home that earns its presence through proportion and material restraint rather than volume. Clean stucco planes, dark window frames, and considered landscaping that reads as intentional from every approach. The interiors carry the same discipline — warm tones grounded by natural light and a palette that doesn't compete with the views.

Salt Spring Island Cabin

Glass, Timber, and Island Light

A cabin scaled to its clearing. Oversized glazing panels frame the surrounding Garry oak and arbutus like a series of curated views — each window a deliberate composition. Early morning. Island light low and warm, catching the grain of exposed timber framing.

Peak Ventures — Whistler

Mountain Modernism

Clean lines against a complex landscape. The flat roofline and dark cladding create a deliberate horizontal plane that contrasts with the vertical thrust of the surrounding conifers. Dark stained cedar, blackened steel, stone. Each surface absorbs or reflects differently, giving the facade depth and weight that shifts with the light.
