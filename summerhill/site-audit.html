Search visibility.
Where you rank today.

I analyzed summerhill.build for technical SEO, on-page optimization, and content strategy. The site has a clean foundation but almost zero search engine optimization in place.

Technical SEO
✓

HTTPS is properly configured. No mixed content issues detected.
✓
Mobile Responsive

!
Page Speed Needs Attention
Heavy image files without next-gen formats (WebP). No lazy loading detected on project pages. This impacts both user experience and Google ranking.

No evidence of a sitemap submitted to Google Search Console. Google may not be discovering all project pages.
!
Schema Markup is Basic
Only generic WebPage and Organization schema. No LocalBusiness, Project, or Review schema. Missing structured data that helps Google understand what Summerhill does and where.
On-Page SEO
✗
Meta Descriptions Auto-Generated
No custom meta descriptions on project pages. Google is pulling random text snippets instead of crafted descriptions that sell the click.
✗
No Keyword Strategy Detected
No targeting for high-value terms like "Sunshine Coast custom home builder," "passive house builder BC," or "luxury home builder Gibsons." These terms have search volume and low competition.
!
Thin Project Page Content
The Perch has a narrative description, but most other projects have minimal text. Google needs 300+ words of unique content per page to rank effectively.
✗
No Internal Linking Strategy
Project pages don’t cross-link to services, about, or related projects. Internal links are one of the strongest on-page SEO signals and they’re completely missing.
!
Image Alt Tags Are Generic
Alt text on project images doesn’t include location or architectural keywords. Every image is a missed opportunity to rank in Google Image search.
Content & Keywords
✗
No Blog or Journal
Zero blog posts. No organic content strategy driving search traffic. Every visitor must find summerhill.build directly or through social. A monthly blog post targeting "custom home building Sunshine Coast" would compound over 12 months.
✗
No Service Area Pages
No dedicated pages for Gibsons, Sechelt, Roberts Creek, Pender Harbour, or Halfmoon Bay. Each of these locations is a separate search market where Summerhill could rank.
✗
Zero Thought Leadership Content
Summerhill builds passive houses and uses CLT. There’s no content explaining these methods to prospective clients. "What is a passive house?" and "CLT construction BC" are searchable topics with almost no competition locally.

SEO Bottom Line

summerhill.build is invisible to Google for the search terms that matter. A prospect searching "custom home builder Sunshine Coast" will find competitors before they find Summerhill. The fix is achievable: targeted project pages, a monthly blog, local keyword optimization, and proper technical setup. This is a 6-month project that compounds permanently.

Local SEO & GEO Audit

Local visibility.
Can they find you on the map?

Local SEO determines whether Summerhill shows up when someone searches "custom builder near me" on the Sunshine Coast. This is where the biggest gap exists.

Google Business Profile
✗
No Google Business Profile Found
Summerhill Fine Homes does not appear to have an active, optimized Google Business Profile. This is the single most important local SEO asset. Without it, you cannot appear in Google Maps results or the Local Pack (the map section at the top of search results).
✗
Not in Google Maps Local Pack
Searching "custom home builder Sunshine Coast" or "builder Gibsons BC" does not surface Summerhill in the map results. This is where 46% of local search clicks go.
✗
Zero Google Reviews
No Google reviews found. Reviews are the #1 ranking factor for local search. Competitors with 10+ reviews will always outrank a business with none.
Local Citations & Directories
!
Limited Directory Presence
No verified listings found on Houzz, HomeStars, or BuilderTrend. These directories rank well for "builder" searches and provide backlinks that boost your main site.
!
NAP Consistency Unknown
Your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) should be identical across all online listings. Inconsistencies confuse Google and hurt local rankings.
✗
No CHBA or Industry Directory Listing
Not listed in CHBA BC or local builder association directories. These are authoritative backlinks that signal credibility to Google.
Service Area Coverage
✗
No Location-Specific Landing Pages
Summerhill serves Gibsons, Sechelt, Roberts Creek, Halfmoon Bay, Pender Harbour, and potentially Squamish/Whistler. None of these have dedicated pages. Each location page is a chance to rank for "[location] custom builder."
✗
No Local Content Strategy
No content about Sunshine Coast building conditions, regulations, or architectural context. Articles like "Building a Custom Home on the Sunshine Coast: What to Know" would capture high-intent search traffic.

Local SEO Bottom Line

The Google Business Profile is the highest-priority fix. It’s free, it takes 30 minutes to set up, and it immediately makes Summerhill visible on Google Maps. Combined with 5-10 Google reviews from past clients, this single action would change your local visibility within 60 days. Everything else builds from there.
