SEO Audit

Search visibility.
What’s working and what’s not.

I ran a technical audit on summerhill.build and crawled 30 pages. The site has a strong foundation with good meta data and fast response times, but there’s one critical gap and a major image SEO issue holding it back.

What’s Working
✓
Valid SSL Certificate
Site is served over HTTPS with no mixed content issues.
✓
Strong Meta Title
"Inspired Building - Summerhill Fine Homes" (41 characters). Clear, branded, good length.
✓
Meta Description Present
"Custom home builder specializing in sustainability and performance" (137 chars). Solid description that communicates the value proposition.
✓
Single H1 Tag
"Inspired Building" — clean hierarchy, no duplicate H1s.
✓
Open Graph Tags
og:title and og:image present. Social sharing will display correctly.
✓
Fast Server Response
78ms to first byte. Well-optimized hosting.
✓
Sitemap Exists
sitemap_index.xml found with 8 URLs indexed. Google can discover your pages.
✓
Canonical Tags
Proper canonical URLs set. No duplicate content issues.
What Needs Fixing
✗
No Structured Data (Schema.org)
Zero JSON-LD markup found on the site. This is the single biggest SEO gap. Adding LocalBusiness, Organization, and Project schema would help Google understand what Summerhill does, where you operate, and what you’ve built. This directly impacts local search visibility and rich snippets.
✗
1,435 Images Missing Alt Text
Of 1,637 total images across the site, 87.6% have no alt text. The homepage alone has 35 of 50 images (70%) without alt tags. This is a significant accessibility and image SEO issue. Every image is a missed opportunity to rank in Google Image search for terms like “custom home Sunshine Coast.”
!
Only 8 URLs in Sitemap
30 crawlable pages but only 8 in the sitemap. The remaining 22 pages may not be getting indexed efficiently. Expanding the sitemap to include all project pages would improve discoverability.

SEO Bottom Line

The site’s technical foundation is solid — fast, secure, well-structured meta data. The two critical fixes are structured data (schema markup for LocalBusiness + Projects) and image alt text across 1,435 images. These are both one-time fixes that permanently improve search visibility.

Local SEO & GEO Audit

Local visibility.
The biggest gap.

Local SEO determines whether Summerhill shows up when someone searches “custom builder near me” on the Sunshine Coast. This is where the most significant gaps exist.

Google Business Profile
✓
Google Business Profile Is Active
Summerhill has a live, active Google Business Profile. This is the foundation for local search visibility and Google Maps presence. Ahead of most Sunshine Coast builders on this front.
!
GBP Optimization Opportunity
Verify that the profile has complete service areas, business categories, photos, and regular posts. An optimized GBP with 10+ reviews significantly outranks a basic listing.
✗
No LocalBusiness Schema on Website
No structured data connecting your website to your GBP. Adding LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema reinforces your local presence and helps Google connect the two.
Directory Presence
✓
Listed on Houzz
Summerhill has a Houzz profile. This is a strong authoritative backlink for residential builders.
!
Not Found on HomeStars
No HomeStars profile detected. HomeStars ranks well for “builder” searches in BC and provides a trust signal plus backlink.
!
Not Found on CHBA Directory
No CHBA BC directory listing detected. This is an authoritative industry backlink that signals credibility to Google. Also required for Georgie Award eligibility.
!
NAP Consistency Unverified
Your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) should be identical across your website footer, Google Business Profile, Houzz, and all other listings. Inconsistencies confuse Google and hurt local rankings.

Local SEO Bottom Line

The GBP is a strong start. The next steps are optimizing it with regular photo uploads, requesting Google reviews from past clients (target 10+), and adding LocalBusiness schema to the website so Google connects your GBP to your site. The directory gaps (HomeStars, CHBA) are secondary but worth filling for citation building.
