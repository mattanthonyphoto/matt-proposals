Where You Stand Today

A quick look at
what’s working and what’s not.

Before proposing anything, I looked at Summerhill’s current online presence, your competitors’ positioning, and where the gaps are. Here’s what I found.

Website

  • Clean design, solid foundation. The Bricks-built site looks professional and the project grid is well-organized across Fine Homes, Renovations, and Commercial.
  • Project pages lack depth. The Perch has 15+ images and architect credits, but no specs, no sq ft, no build timeline, no performance data. Other projects have even less.
  • No blog or content strategy. No blog section found. Zero organic content driving search traffic. Every visitor must find you directly.
  • No testimonials or social proof. 30+ years of building, but no client quotes, no Google reviews referenced, no press mentions on the site.
  • SEO is minimal. Basic schema markup exists but meta descriptions appear auto-generated. No keyword strategy targeting “Sunshine Coast custom builder” or “Passive House builder BC.”

Content and Social

  • Instagram exists but underperforms. Posts are sporadic. A 17-person company with passive house builds and CLT construction should be generating 3-4 posts per week from job sites alone.
  • No video content. Zero project films, zero reels, zero build documentation video. In 2026, video is the highest-engagement format on every platform.
  • Build process content is untapped. Summerhill does CLT, passive house, and mass timber. These are visually compelling construction methods that architects, designers, and homeowners love to watch. None of it is being captured.
  • The team story is hidden. 17+ employees, mentorship programs, community sponsorships, a soccer team. This is the kind of content that builds trust and differentiates, and it’s buried on one About page.

Awards and Credibility

  • Zero awards submitted. 30+ years of building, passive house principles, and design-forward projects with zero Georgie submissions, zero CHBA entries, zero publication features.
  • No certifications displayed. Passive house is referenced but no formal certifications are listed. No LEED, no Built Green, no Energy Star visible on the site.
  • Competitors are pulling ahead. RDC Fine Homes holds 17 Georgie Awards. Other Sea-to-Sky builders are getting featured in Western Living and Mountain Life. Summerhill’s work matches or exceeds this calibre, but the credentials don’t reflect it.

The Bottom Line

Summerhill’s work is excellent. The online presence doesn’t match. The website is clean but thin on content. The social channels are quiet. The award case is empty. The good news: the raw material is already there. You have 30 years of builds, a team worth showcasing, and construction methods that sell themselves on camera. What’s missing is the documentation system to turn all of that into marketing assets.
