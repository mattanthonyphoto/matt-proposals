The Audit

What the data says.
Verified, not estimated.

I crawled 30 pages on summerhill.build and checked your SEO, local presence, content, and visual assets. Here’s what I found.

What’s Strong

  • Technical SEO scores 85/100. Valid SSL, 78ms server response, proper meta titles and descriptions, Open Graph tags, canonical URLs, sitemap with 8 indexed URLs.
  • Content scores 100/100. 20 project pages all with 100+ words of real description. 473-word homepage. This is significantly better than most builders who have image-only portfolios.
  • Visual depth is real. 1,637 total images, averaging 70.7 per project page. Strong visual coverage that most competitors can’t match.
  • Houzz listing active. Authoritative directory backlink already in place.

The Specific Gaps

  • 1,435 images missing alt text (87.6%). Google can’t see or index images without alt text. Every one of those is invisible to Google Image search, which is a major discovery channel for architectural builders.
  • No structured data (schema.org). Zero JSON-LD markup. Google doesn’t know Summerhill is a builder, where you’re located, or what services you offer. This is the #1 technical fix for search visibility.
  • No Google Business Profile. Can’t appear in Google Maps or the Local Pack. 46% of local search clicks go to the map section. Summerhill is invisible there.
  • No video content anywhere. Zero project films, zero reels, zero build documentation video. In 2026, this is the highest-engagement content format and Summerhill has none.
  • Local competitors are pulling ahead. Lincoln Construction in Gibsons is already a Golden Angle and Homes Across Canada award finalist. Jenkins Construction has a polished portfolio with professional photography. Summerhill’s work matches or exceeds this calibre, but the credentials don’t reflect it.

The Bottom Line

Summerhill’s website foundation is ahead of the competition. The gaps are specific, fixable, and high-ROI: structured data, alt text, Google Business Profile, video, and awards. These aren’t rebuild-the-site problems. They’re activate-what-you-have problems. That’s exactly what a Creative Partner does.
