Featured Project

Halfmoon Bay Cabin

Architect Frits de Vries
Size 1,450 sq ft
Location Halfmoon Bay

A Japanese ryokan-inspired retreat on a one-acre waterfront lot. Designed by Patrick Warren of Frits de Vries Architects as a personal sanctuary — where 1970s American coastal architecture meets the restraint of a Kyushu inn.

Clad in black Western Red Cedar with Sansin SDF Onyx finish, the cabin recedes into the forest canopy. Inside, lye-processed cedar creates a luminous contrast — dark without, light within. At 1,450 square feet, it proves that craft and intention matter more than scale.

The Approach

One builder.
One vision.

Kurt Vernon acts as project manager, contract administrator, superintendent, and field engineer on every build. One point of contact from first evaluation to final walkthrough — no layers, no handoffs.

Vernon Construction operates on a cost-plus model — full transparency on every dollar, no hidden markups, no surprises. Open books, itemized invoices, and the freedom to make material decisions with complete price visibility.

Working alongside a trusted network of architects, designers, and skilled trades across the Sunshine Coast, we build homes that respond to their site, their owners, and the way light moves through the day.
